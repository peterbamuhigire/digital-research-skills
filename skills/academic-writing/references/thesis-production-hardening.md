# Thesis Production Hardening

Use this reference for postgraduate theses, dissertations, proposals, and chapter plans.

## Core Thesis Logic

Every thesis or dissertation must expose:

- Research question.
- Rationale and significance.
- Literature gap or dispute.
- Conceptual/theoretical frame.
- Methodology and methods.
- Evidence or data.
- Analysis.
- Contribution to knowledge.
- Limitations.
- Implications.

## Proposal Checks

- The proposal defines a project; it is not a standard essay.
- The question is answerable within time, data access, ethics, and word-count limits.
- Literature review identifies a gap or conflict.
- Methodology is justified against alternatives.
- Limitations are specific.
- Milestones are deliverables, not vague dates.

## Methodology Justification

For each method, state:

- What the method can reveal.
- Why it fits the research question.
- Why alternative methods were rejected.
- What validity, reliability, bias, or transferability issue it creates.
- How those weaknesses will be managed.

## Abstract Logic

The abstract answers:

1. What was done?
2. Why does it matter?
3. What was already known?
4. What was unknown?
5. How was the unknown investigated?
6. What was found or argued?
7. Why does it matter?

## Workflow Guardrails

- Maintain a living outline.
- Track assumptions and unresolved decisions.
- Use versioned files and backups.
- Write to milestones: proposal, ethics, data, analysis, chapter drafts, revision, submission.
- Keep signposting visible so an examiner can follow the argument.

## Ship Gate

- [ ] Research question controls the whole document.
- [ ] Literature review justifies the project.
- [ ] Methodology is defended, not merely described.
- [ ] Contribution is explicit and proportionate.
- [ ] Limitations are credible.
- [ ] Chapter sequence is logical.
- [ ] Viva-defensible countercase is prepared.
