# Universal Methods of Design Router

This reference lets the engine use Bruce Hanington and Bella Martin's *The Pocket Universal Methods of Design* as a method-selection catalogue for complex research, innovation, product discovery, service design, policy design, and evidence-backed recommendations.

Use this as a router only. Do not reproduce book text. Select methods by research purpose, then define the artifact the method must produce.

## Core Rule

Do not say "use design methods" generically. Pick 2-5 methods that fit the research question, evidence gap, timeline, participants, and output decision.

Every selected method must state:

- research question it answers;
- participant, source, or artifact needed;
- output artifact;
- validation or triangulation partner;
- risk or limitation.

## Combination Patterns

### Problem Discovery

1. Secondary Research or Literature Reviews.
2. Contextual Inquiry or Observation.
3. Interviews or Directed Storytelling.
4. Affinity Diagramming or Thematic Networks.
5. Triangulation.

### Service or Journey Diagnosis

1. Stakeholder Maps.
2. User Journey Maps or Scenario Description Swimlanes.
3. Critical Incident Technique.
4. Customer Experience Audit.
5. Weighted Matrix for prioritised interventions.

### Innovation Concept Development

1. Generative Research.
2. Creative Toolkits or Cultural Probes.
3. Design Workshops or Design Charette.
4. Storyboards or Scenarios.
5. Parallel Prototyping.

### Evidence-Backed Recommendation

1. Case Studies or Secondary Research.
2. Interviews or Surveys.
3. Competitive Testing or Evidence-Based Design.
4. Weighted Matrix.
5. A/B Testing, RITE, or Usability Testing where a prototype exists.

### Digital Product or Website Research

1. Content Inventory & Audit.
2. Site Search Analytics or Web Analytics.
3. Cognitive Walkthrough or Heuristic Evaluation.
4. Usability Testing with Think-Aloud Protocol.
5. A/B Testing after a candidate improvement exists.

## Selection Gate

- The methods answer different questions; they are not duplicates.
- At least one method captures real behaviour or real artifacts, not only opinion.
- At least one method supports synthesis or prioritisation.
- At least one method validates or challenges the likely recommendation.
- The method burden fits the project timeline and access constraints.

## Anti-Patterns

- Choosing fashionable methods instead of question-fit methods.
- Running workshops before doing enough discovery.
- Treating surveys as proof when the issue requires observed behaviour.
- Treating interviews as representative market evidence.
- Producing maps or personas without source traceability.
- Testing a solution before defining the problem.
- Using all 100 methods as a checklist. The catalogue is for selection, not exhaustion.
