# Reference - Oxbridge / Ivy / LSE Examination Conventions

**Canonical sources:** current published institutional regulations and guidance listed in the source register below. Tier 1: institutional primary sources.

**Accessed:** 2026-04-26.

Use this reference when drafting, reviewing, or packaging a doctoral dissertation, DPhil thesis, PhD thesis, MPhil thesis, MLitt thesis, or equivalent research-degree artefact for Cambridge, Oxford, LSE, Harvard, Yale, Princeton, or an institution being benchmarked against them.

## Core rule

Do not encode a single "top institution" word count. The examined standard is institution- and subject-specific.

Where the target institution is known, apply its current published rule. Where the target department or programme is unknown, use the conservative benchmark below and flag the missing programme rule as a gap.

## Conservative benchmark for unknown department

- UK/Oxbridge/LSE doctoral thesis: default planning ceiling 80,000 words unless the institutional or subject regulation permits 100,000 words.
- LSE PhD: hard planning ceiling 100,000 words including footnotes, excluding bibliography and appendices, unless permission to exceed has been granted.
- Harvard/Yale/Princeton PhD: no universal word-count ceiling was found in the central graduate-school guidance reviewed for this pass. Use department/programme rules where published; otherwise design around field norms, committee expectations, and the required dissertation standard.
- If an artefact contains papers/articles, require an integrating introduction, literature/context frame, contribution statement, and conclusion/synthesis unless the department explicitly says otherwise.
- Treat all word counts as maxima, not targets. Several regulations state or imply that successful theses may be shorter.

## Cambridge

### Word-count rule

Cambridge word limits are set by Degree Committee. The central Cambridge student guidance says theses must comply with the relevant Degree Committee limit; a non-compliant thesis may be refused or returned before examination.

Common PhD patterns in the current Cambridge page:

- Many humanities and social-science PhDs: 80,000 words.
- Biology / clinical medicine / chemistry / physics and related laboratory sciences: often 60,000 words, with some special-permission routes to 80,000 words.
- Engineering: 65,000 words, with specified inclusions/exclusions.
- Law: 80,000 words excluding footnotes, appendices, and bibliography, subject to an overall 100,000-word limit excluding bibliography, table of contents, and preliminary matter.
- Economics: 60,000 words including tables, footnotes, bibliography, and appendices.
- Mathematics: no standard format; candidate must discuss topic-appropriate format with supervisor.

### Structural and examination requirements

Cambridge expects a thesis to include critical review and the results of the candidate's original investigation. Published material may be included only as part of a connected argument with introductory and concluding passages. For thesis-by-papers or published-work formats, the thesis must include a substantial introductory summary statement explaining rationale, field context, coherence, original contribution to knowledge, and the candidate's exact contribution to each paper.

The examination consists of thesis submission and examination in the general field. The viva should allow the candidate to defend the thesis, clarify matters raised by examiners, demonstrate knowledge of the field, clarify authorship/collaboration, and enable examiners to reach a definite outcome.

For the PhD under Special Regulations, Cambridge requires published work to make a significant contribution to scholarship through new knowledge, connecting previously unrelated facts, theory development, or revision of older views; show research technique and advanced-enquiry understanding; and be equivalent in quality and quantity to three years of full-time postgraduate study. Applicants submit an introductory summary of 1,000-5,000 words and published supporting works.

## Oxford

### Word-count rule

Oxford's central research-examination page directs students to the specific Examination Regulations for their course or subject. The DPhil general regulations do not create one universal word count; subject regulations do.

Current examples:

- Economics DPhil: 100,000-word maximum excluding bibliography; MLitt 50,000; MSc by Research 25,000. Integrated thesis may consist of at least three publishable-quality papers framed by an introduction, literature survey, and conclusion; at least one paper must be sole-authored by the candidate.
- Law DPhil: 100,000-word maximum including footnotes and appendices, excluding abstract, table of contents, table of cases, table of statutes, bibliography, headers/footers, and index.
- Humanities Division transfer/confirmation standards require written work around 10,000 words at transfer and confirmation stages, but individual faculty regulations govern final thesis limits.

### Structural and examination requirements

Oxford's DPhil examination regulations require a digital PDF thesis and an abstract of about 300 words placed in the thesis. Examiners consider the thesis and abstract, examine the candidate orally on the thesis, test general knowledge of the field where needed, and report on the scope, character, and quality of the work.

For first submissions on or after Sunday of Week 1, Michaelmas Term 2025, examiners must be satisfied that the student has good general knowledge of the field, has made a significant and substantial contribution, has presented the thesis lucidly and scholarly, merits the DPhil, and has provided a satisfactory abstract.

Oxford's current outcomes include no amendments, minor corrections, major corrections, major corrections plus mandatory viva, reference back options, lower-degree outcomes, and fail. Current correction periods include two months for minor corrections, six months for major corrections, and one year for reference back.

## LSE

### Word-count rule

LSE's school-wide Regulations for Research Degrees state that a PhD thesis must not exceed 100,000 words, including footnotes but excluding bibliography and appendices. The MPhil limit is 60,000 words on the same inclusion/exclusion basis. Permission to exceed the normal length may be granted by the Research Degrees Subcommittee Chair in appropriate circumstances.

### Structural and examination requirements

LSE requires the PhD thesis to form a distinct contribution to knowledge and evidence originality through discovery of new facts and/or independent critical power. It must critically assess relevant literature, describe method and findings, discuss findings, indicate how the thesis advances the subject, demonstrate deep and synoptic understanding of the field, demonstrate research skills, and be of publishable standard in whole, part, or revised form.

LSE permits either a monograph forming an integrated whole or, where the department permits it, a thesis normally consisting of at least three papers. Published or jointly authored work may be included only where it is integral to the thesis, contributes to the main theme, is in the same format as the rest of the thesis, and clearly states the candidate's contribution.

The LSE examination is thesis plus oral examination, normally in English. The oral may be at LSE or by approved video conferencing. Examiners prepare independent preliminary reports before the oral, then a joint final report after the oral. The supervisor may attend as observer unless the candidate indicates otherwise, but does not have the right to participate unless invited by examiners and agreed by the candidate.

## Harvard

### Word-count rule

No central Harvard Griffin GSAS dissertation word-count ceiling was found in the current central formatting guidance reviewed for this pass. Harvard word-count/page expectations are programme- or department-specific where they exist.

Examples:

- Harvard SEAS states that the dissertation must be a coherent document, not merely a collection of manuscripts, and must represent an original and significant contribution to knowledge.
- Harvard Medical School PhD guidance requires original treatment of a fitting subject, scholarly review of pertinent literature, independent research, clear/logical/careful writing, Introduction and Conclusion chapters, and contribution statements for collaborative work.
- Harvard Health Policy permits three-paper or monograph formats and requires scholarly style, thorough literature reviews, detailed descriptions of methods/data/analyses, an overview for three-paper formats, non-technical implications, future research, and innovation by new application, new problem, or method extension/modification.

### Structural and examination requirements

Harvard dissertation structure must be checked at the school/programme level. The common current pattern is: original and significant contribution, coherent scholarly document, field-appropriate literature review, detailed methods, explicit authorship/contribution statements for collaborative work, public presentation or defense component, and final GSAS formatting compliance.

For SEAS, the final oral exam is a presentation and defense of the dissertation, with broader field questions permitted. A complete penultimate draft is due to the research committee at least two weeks before the final examination.

For Health Policy, a draft dissertation is due to committee members at least two weeks before the defense; all defenses have a public presentation component; the committee may keep the examination component public or close it.

## Yale

### Word-count rule

No universal Yale GSAS dissertation word-count ceiling was found in the central degree-requirements and formatting guidance reviewed for this pass. Yale states that faculty apply prevailing intellectual standards and scholarly practices in their fields to suitable scope, length, and structure.

Departmental examples can impose page or structure expectations. For example, Yale MCDB describes a typical dissertation as 100-250 pages and normally containing at least two first-authored research chapters plus general introduction and final discussion/integration chapters.

### Structural and examination requirements

Yale GSAS states that a dissertation should demonstrate mastery of relevant resources and methods and make an original contribution to knowledge. It normally has a single topic, broadly defined, with interrelated parts that may be discrete units.

Yale central formatting guidance requires PDF submission, double spacing, single-sided layout, one-inch margins, 10- to 12-point font, and this section order: abstract, title page, copyright page, table of contents, front matter, body of text, back matter.

Departmental defenses vary. Yale Computer Science requires an oral defense when the committee is satisfied that the work is complete and a complete draft is ready; the defense has a one-hour public presentation, a 15-minute public question/discussion period, and a closed oral examination by faculty/outside readers. Final submission must occur within one month of passing the defense or the student must defend again.

## Princeton

### Word-count rule

No universal Princeton Graduate School dissertation word-count ceiling was found in the current Graduate School dissertation/FPO and submission guidance reviewed for this pass. Departmental handbooks may set limits. For example, Princeton Near Eastern Studies says any dissertation exceeding 100,000 words will be returned unless advance permission has been obtained.

### Structural and examination requirements

Princeton Graduate School states that the dissertation must show technical mastery of the field and capability for independent and original research. It must enlarge or modify current knowledge in a field or present a significant new interpretation of known materials.

At least two principal readers are required, with at least one from the student's home department. Each principal reader submits a written and signed reader report. A copy of the dissertation to be defended must be available in the department before the Final Public Oral.

The Final Public Oral is both an examination in the student's field and a defense of the dissertation. It occurs after Graduate School review/acceptance of the advanced degree application. The date, time, and place must be posted for at least three days to preserve the public character of the examination. There must be at least three principal examiners, at least two of whom have not been principal readers. The advanced degree application is due at least three weeks before the FPO and includes a correctly formatted title page and abstract of 350 words or fewer, plus reader reports submitted by the department.

## Encoding rules for the engine

1. Ask for or infer the exact institution, school, department, degree, and dissertation format before applying a word-count rule.
2. If Cambridge or Oxford: never rely on a university-wide PhD/DPhil count. Load the exact Degree Committee / Examination Regulation.
3. If LSE PhD: default to 100,000 words including footnotes and excluding bibliography and appendices.
4. If Harvard/Yale/Princeton: search the central graduate-school guidance first, then the exact school/department handbook. If no word count is published, state "no published central word-count cap found" and use structural/examiner standards instead of fabricating a number.
5. For thesis-by-papers: require a coherent whole, field context/literature review, explicit candidate contribution statements for co-authored work, and synthetic conclusion.
6. For all six institutions: require explicit original-contribution language, methodology justification, literature-review/gap logic, publication-quality findings, and viva/defense readiness.
7. For all source claims: cite the exact institutional page/PDF at point of use. If the relevant department rule cannot be found, mark it as a gap.
