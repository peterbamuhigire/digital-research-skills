# Five-term source doubt

Abbott: *"You are the reader's agent, and you must be worthy of that trust."* Every primary document must answer five questions before it earns the right to be cited as evidence.

## The five terms (Burke pentad, adapted)

### 1. Author
- Who wrote / produced / signed it?
- What is their position, interest, expertise?
- What is their relation to the events described?
- What incentives / pressures shaped what they wrote?

### 2. Provenance
- Where has the document been since creation?
- Through whose hands has it passed?
- Has it been edited, redacted, translated?
- What is the chain of custody?

### 3. Production
- When was it produced?
- Under what conditions? (Wartime? Crisis? Routine?)
- For what immediate purpose?
- In what genre / format / register?

### 4. Mechanics
- What is the medium? (Print, manuscript, digital, oral)
- What language / dialect / register?
- What is the layout / structure / paratext?
- What might the form itself be telling us? (Letterhead, stamp, signature, censor's mark)

### 5. Aims
- What was the document **intended** to do?
- Who was its intended audience?
- What audience reaction would have served the author's purposes?
- What is the document **not** saying that one might expect?

## Decision rules

- **Apply to every primary source before quoting.** Failure makes the citation hollow.
- **Apply lightly to secondary sources.** The five-term doubt was designed for primary; secondary scholarship has its own evaluation rubric (see `tudor-twelve-point-evaluation`).
- **Document the answers.** A source citation should travel with at least a one-line summary of who/what/when/why.
- **Surface gaps.** "Author unknown" is a real answer; record it as such.
- **Watch the aims-vs-content gap.** When the document's apparent content doesn't match what its aims would predict, that's a finding.

## Worked example — Daily Monitor 2 Aug 2023 deposit-non-refund piece

| Term | Answer |
|---|---|
| Author | Daily Monitor staff reporter (specific byline if available) |
| Provenance | Published online; URL stable; archived |
| Production | August 2023; Uganda; routine investigative coverage; post-L&T-Act-2022 context |
| Mechanics | Digital long-read; English; embeds quotes from named tenants and lawyers |
| Aims | Inform Ugandan public; pressure landlords / regulators; also drive subscriptions |

This doesn't undermine the 80% statistic — it locates it. The number is reliable for its purpose; readers should know that purpose.

## What this is not

- It is not a tier-grading. Tiers come from `source-verification`.
- It is not a 12-point evaluation. That's `tudor-twelve-point-evaluation` (for media especially).
- It is not a credibility score. It produces a structured note about the document's situatedness.

## Anti-patterns

- Skipping for "obvious" documents (often the most dangerous to skip)
- Treating the answer "unknown" as license to ignore — note it
- Doing it after extracting quotes (do it first)
- One-shot — the five terms can change if new info about the document emerges
