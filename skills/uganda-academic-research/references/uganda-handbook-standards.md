## Uganda Default Scaffold

Use this only when the user has not named the target Ugandan institution. Mark it as a synthesis from the handbooks.

- Concept paper: concise problem, purpose/objectives, significance, theoretical/conceptual frame, short literature review, methodology, and references.
- Proposal: preliminary pages, Chapter One/Introduction, Chapter Two/Literature Review, Chapter Three/Methodology, references, appendices, work plan, and budget where required.
- Final report/dissertation/thesis: preliminary pages, introduction, literature review, methodology, findings/results, discussion, conclusions/recommendations, references, appendices.
- Citation: APA is the safest default for social-science and multidisciplinary work unless the faculty prescribes another style. Busitema also notes Harvard/numerical variants by discipline.
- Ethics: route human-subject, privacy, physical/psychological wellbeing, or sensitive-fieldwork studies through the relevant Research Ethics Committee before data collection.

## Concept Papers

Busitema: concept papers must not exceed five pages. The mined outline includes title, background, problem, objectives, research questions/hypotheses, significance, theoretical/conceptual framework, literature review, methodology, and references.

UCU: graduate-level students are advised to have a supervisor-approved concept paper before the proposal stage. UCU also gives a five-page maximum for the concept paper.

## Proposal Structure

Makerere: a proposal is an intended research plan covering problem, purpose, scope/objectives, methodology, references, and financial plan/budget. A synopsis is 3-5 pages including references. Master's degree research proposals should be up to 15 pages. PhD proposals should not exceed 25 pages. The proposal title should not be more than 20 words.

Busitema: the proposal has preliminary pages, main body, and appendices. The main body is organized as Chapter One Introduction, Chapter Two Literature Review, and Chapter Three Methodology.

UCU: proposal lengths are PhD 25-45 pages, Master's 15-30 pages, Postgraduate Diploma 15-30 pages, and Bachelor's 10-20 pages. UCU proposal text uses Trebuchet MS 12, double spacing, one-inch margins, and page numbers at bottom center.

## Final Research Document

Busitema: the research report has five sections: preliminary pages, main body, data presentation and analysis, discussion/findings/summary/conclusions, and appendices. Its abstract should preferably not exceed 300 words.

Makerere: research reports, theses, and dissertations include preliminary pages, body/text, bibliography, and appendices. The abstract/executive summary should preferably not exceed 300 words. Manuscript preparation follows APA Sixth Edition unless the discipline has an approved alternative.

UCU: research-output lengths refer to the main body and exclude preliminaries, references/bibliography, footnotes/endnotes, and appendices. UCU lists Doctoral Thesis 30,000-100,000 words, Postgraduate Dissertation 15,000-25,000 words, Undergraduate Dissertation 10,000-15,000 words, and Reports/Extended Essays 5,000-10,000 words. The same manual also states that a PhD length is 80,000-100,000 words; treat this as an internal handbook tension and ask which faculty/school rule governs before enforcing a PhD lower bound.

UCU abstract limits: PhD thesis not more than 350 words, Master's dissertation not more than 250 words, Bachelor's dissertation not more than 150 words, and other research reports not more than 100 words.

## Chapter Structures

Common Ugandan scaffold:

1. Introduction: background, problem statement, purpose/aim, objectives, questions/hypotheses, scope, significance, conceptual/theoretical framework, and chapter synopsis where required.
2. Literature Review: theoretical and empirical literature, gap identification, source currency, and alignment to objectives.
3. Methodology: research design, area/location, population, sampling, data collection, validity/reliability or trustworthiness, data analysis, ethics, and constraints.
4. Findings / Results / Data Presentation: systematic presentation aligned to objectives, questions, and hypotheses.
5. Discussion: interpretation against literature, controversies, discoveries, limitations, and contribution.
6. Conclusions and Recommendations: conclusions tied to objectives/findings, practical implications, recommendations, and further research.

UCU's assessment rubric gives weight to structure/presentation, introduction, literature review, methodology, data presentation/analysis, discussion, conclusion/recommendations, references, and appendices. UCU also asks literature-review reviewers to check for at least 30 quality references, original sources, balance of source types, source currency, and clear research gap.

## Citation, Plagiarism, and Ethics

Makerere: ethics requires attention to minimal abuse/disregard, safety, and social/psychological wellbeing of persons, communities, and animals. Ethical clearance must be stated where applicable.

Busitema: all sources used in proposals/final-year reports must be cited in text and in the reference list. The handbook describes Harvard referencing as a recommended style in its guide while also noting APA and numerical styles.

UCU: APA is the default documentation style unless a faculty requires and communicates a specific alternative. Proposals requiring ethical approval go to the Research Ethics Committee with supervisor/faculty guidance. Ethical considerations include confidentiality, consent, assent, anonymity, integrity, and benevolence.

## Viva and Examination

UCU: viva/oral defence eligibility requires favourable evaluation by at least one internal and one external examiner. If examiner opinions diverge significantly, additional examiners and/or an arbitrator may be appointed. Oral defence presentations should be structured; UCU recommends 15 minutes for Master's and 45 minutes for doctoral candidates. The viva panel includes the Dean of the School of Research and Postgraduate Studies or representative as chair, faculty dean, head of department, supervisor representatives, and an expert nominated by the faculty/department.

UCU pass-mark note: the extracted examiner-report rubric states an overall pass mark of 60% for Master's and 70% for doctoral theses. Confirm current faculty rules before using this as a final award threshold.
