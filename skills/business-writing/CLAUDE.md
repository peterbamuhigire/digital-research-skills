# Claude-specific — business-writing

- Pick the channel before drafting; load only the matching reference.
- Run all seven Maxwell steps; never skip; never collapse 6 into 5 or 7 into 6.
- Apply "don't mumble" as a top-level filter; remove hedge stacks.
- Strike Latinate substitutes whenever an Anglo-Saxon word fits.
- Replace vague adjectives with numbers, names, or dates.
- Lead every artifact with the news; never bury the recommendation.
- For multi-language artifacts, load `references/language-standards-en-fr-sw.md` and apply per-language register rules.
- For hard scenarios (bad news, decline, apology, discourteous reply, policy change), load `references/scenario-playbooks.md` first and resolve the four axes (background, purpose, audience, communication strategy) **before** drafting.
- For pitch decks, one-pagers, training material, business plans, grant proposals, or performance appraisals, load `references/extended-channels.md` and apply the matching section.
- Whenever an LLM is in the production chain, load `references/ai-assisted-writing.md` and embed the engine's evidence-discipline clause verbatim in every prompt that drafts shippable text. Treat AI citations and statistics as draft claims until verified.
- The Garner / Flowers MACJ four-task model (Madman / Architect / Carpenter / Judge) is a valid working framing of Maxwell's seven steps. Use whichever is closer at hand; do not collapse the discipline.

For reports, proposals, white papers, redirect to `report-and-proposal-craft`.
