## Kenya Default Scaffold

Use only when the user has not named the target Kenyan institution. Mark as synthesis.

- Proposal: concept paper or topic approval first, then proposal with background/problem, objectives/questions/hypotheses, literature review, methodology, ethics/logistics, references, time schedule, budget, and appendices.
- Thesis/dissertation: preliminary pages, Chapter One Introduction, Chapter Two Literature Review, Chapter Three Research Design and Methodology, findings/results, discussion, conclusions/recommendations, references, appendices.
- Citation: APA is the safest default for the supplied Kenyan university handbooks unless the institution prescribes another style.
- Integrity: require originality/plagiarism report where the handbook requires it, and route human-subject work through ethics approval.

## Kenyatta University

Kenyatta University School of Hospitality, Tourism and Leisure Studies proposal rules:

- Proposal length: not more than 20 pages for Master's and 30 pages for PhD, excluding references, appendices, and preliminary pages.
- Presentation format: Times New Roman 12 on A4 paper.
- Proposal abstract: not more than 500 words, single-spaced, one block paragraph, no in-text references, and on one page.
- Title: not more than 20 words.
- Referencing: APA latest edition is the recommended in-text and reference-list style; references are listed alphabetically and spaced at 1.5.
- Plagiarism/originality: summary page required; similarity index maximum 25%.
- Thesis page expectations: MSc thesis minimum 80 pages and PhD thesis minimum 120 pages, excluding references and appendices.
- Submission planning: students submit Notice of Thesis Submission within three months of thesis submission; failure to submit by the proposed date requires re-submission of notice.
- Publication readiness: students attach evidence of intent to submit an article for publication in a refereed journal.

Kenyatta University chapter expectations:

- Literature review should be structured around objectives, identify gaps, avoid plagiarism, and use current refereed journals and periodicals. Unless archival, 80% of references should be less than five years old.
- Chapter Three must justify every methodological choice/action, including design, variables, location, target population, sampling, data collection, reliability/validity, data analysis, logistical considerations, and ethical considerations.
- Thesis Chapter Four presents findings against hypotheses and research questions. Results should be segmented by subheadings and stated clearly before detailed evidence.
- Discussion emphasizes academic argumentation and interpretation of results against the research problem.

## Adventist University of Africa

AUA distinguishes project, thesis, and dissertation:

- Project: applied, may include a manual, handbook, training/workshop, or software product/tool for MSc Applied Computer Science. Typical project length is 70-80 pages excluding appendixes; Computer Science project length is 45-80 pages excluding appendixes.
- Thesis: more theoretical, may collect data and measure variables, and may advance theory. It is defended at proposal level and before a three- to six-member Final Oral Defence Panel. Typical thesis length is 90-120 pages excluding appendixes; Computer Science thesis length is 60-90 pages excluding appendixes.
- Dissertation: PhD-level, comprehensive empirical research expected to contribute new knowledge and advance scholarship. It requires passing the Comprehensive Examination before writing, proposal defence, and final defence before a five- to seven-member panel. Typical dissertation length is 200-250 pages excluding appendixes; Computer Science dissertation length is 90-180 pages excluding appendixes.

AUA preliminary pages for projects/theses/dissertations:

1. Abstract, approximately 250-350 words.
2. Title page.
3. Copyright page.
4. Approval page.
5. Dedication page, optional.
6. Table of contents.
7. List of tables if five or more are used.
8. List of figures if five or more are used.
9. List of symbols/abbreviations if five or more are used at least three times each.
10. Acknowledgements, optional.

AUA abstract: describe problem, methodology, findings, and brief conclusions or recommendations; include 3-5 keywords.

AUA plagiarism: Turnitin is used; similarity/originality index should generally be less than 20%. Students should not select repository storage when checking drafts. Plagiarism penalties may include paper failure, suspension, expulsion, or degree revocation.

AUA ethics: the Institutional Ethics Review Committee reviews human-participant protections, dignity, safety, rights, and wellbeing. Ethics clearance is initiated after adviser/program approval of the paper, consent forms, and instruments.

## Regional Centre on Groundwater Resources

Treat RCGW as research governance and integrity guidance, especially for institutional or commissioned groundwater research.

RCGW researcher duties include:

- Obtain ethics, protocol, standard operating procedure, and other required approvals before research activities that need prior approval.
- Use scholarly and scientific rigor in obtaining, recording, analyzing, reporting, and publishing data/results.
- Avoid duplicate manuscript submission and duplicate data/manuscript publication.
- Avoid misrepresentation of data or images; explain image manipulation where used.
- Avoid arrangements that let vested-interest agencies suppress findings or withhold interpretive information.
- Acknowledge RCGW affiliation in publications resulting from research undertaken while a member of the Centre.

RCGW Principal Investigator duties include:

- Plan and coordinate work according to the approved work plan.
- Ensure team compliance with research guidelines.
- Execute the funded proposal using sound management.
- Manage the financial plan and report project progress to sponsors.
- Maintain accurate records of project expenses.
- Comply with sponsor rules, regulations, and award terms.

RCGW use-of-others-work rules:

- Do not knowingly plagiarize.
- Obtain permission before using unpublished work or data, even with attribution.
- Do not enter arrangements giving exclusive use of collaborator data without written informed consent.
- Use archival material according to archival-source rules.

RCGW data and publication controls:

- Preserve data location records where limited-access databases prevent PI retention.
- Use peer review, validation, and publication controls for research outputs.
- Protect intellectual property and patentable work.
- Use agreements for collaborative research.

## Ethics and Integrity Gates

- AUA: less than 20% Turnitin similarity/originality index generally expected.
- Kenyatta University SHTLS: originality report summary required; similarity index maximum 25%.
- RCGW: approvals, data integrity, publication integrity, conflict controls, and PI accountability are mandatory governance concerns.
