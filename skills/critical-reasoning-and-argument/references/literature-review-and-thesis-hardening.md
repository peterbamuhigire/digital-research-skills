# Literature Review And Thesis Hardening

Use this reference for academic writing, literature reviews, research proposals, theses, dissertations, and postgraduate research support.

## Literature Review Workflow

1. **Choose the research question.** A review without a question becomes a source catalogue.
2. **Find and evaluate sources.** Record provenance, method, date, discipline, population/context, and relevance.
3. **Immerse in the literature.** Read for concepts, disputes, methods, findings, and limitations.
4. **Build an analytical outline.** Organize by themes, debates, methods, chronology, theory, or findings, not by author list.
5. **Write sections.** Each section needs a claim, synthesis, evidence, and implication for the research question.
6. **Revise for argument.** Remove summaries that do not advance the gap, method, or contribution.

## Analysis-Synthesis Questions

Ask:

- What are the main themes or schools of thought?
- Where do authors agree, disagree, or talk past each other?
- Which methods dominate, and what do those methods miss?
- What concepts are defined differently across the literature?
- What populations, geographies, periods, or cases are over- or under-studied?
- What findings are robust, tentative, contradictory, or obsolete?
- What assumptions do researchers share?
- What evidence would change the debate?
- What gap justifies this project?

## Source Red Flags

Flag:

- Suspicious funding or undeclared conflict.
- Sloppy method or unclear sample.
- Confounders ignored.
- Strong claim from weak design.
- Stale data used for a current claim.
- Web commentary used where peer-reviewed or primary sources are required.
- Citation chain where later writers repeat an unsupported original claim.
- One-source paragraph.
- Source summary with no synthesis.

## Literature Review Standard

A literature review is not an annotated bibliography. It must:

- Define the problem or question.
- Map the intellectual territory.
- Compare and group sources.
- Identify disputes, limits, and gaps.
- Justify the research design or interpretive approach.
- Show why the new project matters.

## Thesis And Dissertation Hardening

Before drafting chapters, establish:

- Central research question.
- Rationale and contribution to knowledge.
- Scope and exclusions.
- Conceptual or theoretical frame.
- Methodology and methods, including why alternatives were rejected.
- Ontology/epistemology where the discipline expects it.
- Ethics, data access, and limitations.
- Chapter logic and signposting.
- Examiner expectations and institutional rules.

## Proposal Mistake Checks

- The proposal is not a standard essay; it must define a doable research project.
- The literature review cannot be mainly blogs, websites, or convenient sources.
- Methodology must be suitable for the question, not chosen because it is familiar.
- Limitations must be explicit; no method is perfect.
- The project must have milestones, not just a calendar.

## Thesis Workflow Guardrails

- Own the project: maintain decisions, assumptions, and unresolved issues.
- Use milestones tied to outputs: proposal, ethics, data collection, analysis, chapter drafts, revision, submission.
- Maintain version control and backups.
- Keep a living outline.
- Draft sections in a productive order; introduction and abstract often firm up later.
- Use signposting so the examiner can see the argument's movement.

## Abstract Logic

A strong postgraduate abstract answers:

1. What was done?
2. Why does it matter?
3. What was already known?
4. What was unknown or unresolved?
5. How was the unknown investigated?
6. What was found or argued?
7. Why does the finding or argument matter?

## Final Academic Reasoning Gate

- [ ] Research question drives every chapter or section.
- [ ] Literature review synthesizes, not lists.
- [ ] Gap and contribution are explicit.
- [ ] Methodology is justified against alternatives.
- [ ] Claims are evidence-calibrated.
- [ ] Counter-literature is handled fairly.
- [ ] Limitations are honest and specific.
- [ ] Signposting makes the argument inspectable.
