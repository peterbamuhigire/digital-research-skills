# MROC design and management

MROCs (Market Research Online Communities) are *purposeful* communities — distinct from natural communities (Facebook) or brand communities. Members are recruited and consent to participate in research over a finite or open-ended period. The format crosses the qual/quant divide.

## Two main shapes

### Short-term MROC
- 3 days to 3 months
- 30–500 members
- Agency-moderated
- Daily activity cycle
- Typical use: concept testing, journey mapping, longitudinal product trial

### Long-term MROC
- 6+ months
- 100–1,200 members
- Often client-moderated
- Weekly cycle
- Typical use: ongoing brand listening, panel-as-asset, hybrid research + advocacy

## Five-step design

### 1. Strategic
- Short-term or long-term?
- Open (anyone can join) or closed (invitation only)?
- Size and demographic targets
- Research-only or hybrid (research + WOM advocacy)?

### 2. Look-and-feel
- Branded vs neutral
- Forum / chat / blog / hybrid platform
- Avatars, profiles, friending features

### 3. Tools
| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Discussions / forums | Asynchronous depth (oldest-first / newest-first / embedded) |
| Polls | Engagement, not measurement |
| Surveys | Quant integration |
| Blogs | Diary mode for individual members |
| Online chat | Synchronous spurts |
| Photo / video upload | Visual evidence |
| Wikis / whiteboards | Co-creation |
| Personal profiles | Identity, friending |

Poynter's warning: *"If too many unnecessary features are included, there is a risk that members may be confused about what they are supposed to be doing."*

### 4. Recruiting
- Source: client database, online access panel, social-network outreach, custom recruit
- Screening criteria explicit and pre-published
- Terms & conditions covering IP ownership (must vest in client), conduct rules, secrecy, ban on offline-contact between members, data protection

### 5. Privacy / safety
- Walled garden default
- Pseudonyms accepted; full names rarely required
- Sensitive-data classes (per ESOMAR guidelines): race, politics, religion, union, sexual preference, health
- Children: COPPA, ICC/ESOMAR child research guidelines

## The community plan

Equivalent to a discussion guide for the whole community. Includes:
- Welcome email + onboarding sequence
- Discussion topic per week, each tied to a research objective
- Polls / surveys schedule
- Online + offline tasks (factory tours, F2F focus groups for select members)
- Closing protocol

## Moderator team

Three-person team typical:
- Junior — day-to-day moderation, member welcomes, content posting
- Mid — discussion facilitation, summarisation
- Senior — insight ownership, cross-community comparison, client liaison

## Incentives

Three open questions:
1. Use them at all? (Some communities run on intrinsic engagement)
2. Cash / points / prizes / intrinsic?
3. Link to: every member / every active member / prize draw / "best post"?

Poynter's warning: *"One trap that some new communities fall into is that they offer very large incentives initially, in order to 'get it going'."* Hard to scale back later.

Typical short-term BBG incentive: ~US$50/week per active member.

## Deliverables

- Day-to-day moderator summaries
- Weekly insight memos
- Mid-project debrief
- Final synthesis report combining qual themes + community polls + member videos
- Member videos: *"one customer making their point clearly on video can have more impact than a chart based on a thousand people."*

## Decision rules

- **Pick short-term or long-term first** — different design, recruiting, pricing
- **Closed by default** — open communities are harder to manage and have weaker data quality
- **Three-person mod team** for any community above 50 members
- **Community plan finalised before recruiting** — recruiting goals depend on plan
- **IP vests in client** — non-negotiable contractually
- **Anonymise quotes in deliverables** — paraphrase-not-quote rule (Poynter ethics)

## Anti-patterns

- Treating MROC as just a longer focus group (it isn't — async + multi-task + multi-modal)
- Tool overload — confusing members, diluting engagement
- Massive opening incentive — sets unsustainable expectation
- Recruiting before the community plan exists
- Quote-as-verbatim from member posts (Poynter: search engines de-anonymise — paraphrase)
- Mixing research + marketing without explicit consent (Category 6 violation)

## Monetisation

- Short-term MROC project fee: typically five-figure USD; six for large international
- Long-term MROC: annual retainer, often US$200k–$1m+ depending on size and frequency
- Hybrid (research + WOM advocacy) communities: ROI argument is **direct** because they generate sales — can subsidise research with marketing budgets
