# Geographic Scope

**Date:** 2026-05-03

## In scope

- **Uganda** — primary
- **Kenya** — secondary, for triangulation
- **Tanzania** — secondary, for triangulation

These three countries are the East African Community states with the deepest published health-data coverage and the most relevant clinical-practice overlap with Uganda. Data anchored to Uganda; Kenya and Tanzania used to corroborate where Uganda data is thin or to fill specific gaps.

## Out of scope

The remaining East African Community states are **out of scope** for primary data unless they appear as comparators within a WHO or IHME multi-country dataset that we are already citing for Uganda/Kenya/Tanzania:

- Rwanda
- Burundi
- South Sudan
- Democratic Republic of the Congo
- Somalia

Burden-of-disease data for these states is referenced only where it is incidental to a WHO/IHME source we are already using for the in-scope three.

## Why this scope

The app is being built for Uganda. Kenya and Tanzania are the natural triangulation set because:

1. They share large parts of Uganda's epidemiological profile (malaria-endemic, high HIV/TB burden, similar maternal/neonatal indicators, comparable NCD trajectories)
2. Their formularies (Kenya EML, Tanzania NEMLIT) overlap substantially with Uganda EMHSLU and WHO EML
3. They have published, accessible HMIS/STG data in English
4. Their regulators (Kenya PPB, Tanzania TMDA) maintain searchable drug registers

## Implications for sub-agent briefing

Every sub-agent brief must restate:

- "Data is **anchored to Uganda**. Use Kenya and Tanzania sources to triangulate or fill gaps."
- "Where Uganda-specific data is unavailable, cite Kenya or Tanzania and **flag explicitly** that the figure is regional, not Ugandan."
- "Do not extrapolate from Kenya or Tanzania to Uganda silently."
