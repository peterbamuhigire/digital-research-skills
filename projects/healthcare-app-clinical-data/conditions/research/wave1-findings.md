# Wave 1 Conditions Cohort — Research Findings

**Date:** 2026-05-03

**Scope:** 220 ICD-10-coded conditions ranked by disease burden (DALY) in Uganda (primary) and Kenya/Tanzania (triangulation). All ages; population-stratified (adult/paeds/neonatal/obstetric/geriatric/all). Excludes veterinary, traditional medicine, cardiothoracic surgery, neurosurgery, transplant surgery. ## Epidemiological Context

### Uganda Disease Burden (Primary)

Uganda's health system is dominated by communicable diseases, maternal-neonatal conditions, and an emerging non-communicable disease (NCD) burden:

- **Malaria**: 48.3% of outpatient diagnoses (101,266 / 209,734 visits) [disease-diagnosis-uganda-2010]
- **Communicable diseases overall**: 76.3% of primary care diagnoses [disease-diagnosis-uganda-2010]
- **Neonatal conditions** (prematurity, birth asphyxia, neonatal infections): leading cause of facility deaths (9.4% FY 2023/24) [moh-uganda-ahspr-2024]
- **Pneumonia**: 8.2% of facility deaths; 3.9% of outpatient diagnoses [moh-uganda-ahspr-2024; disease-diagnosis-uganda-2010]
- **Non-communicable diseases**: 33% of hospital admissions, 11% of deaths (rising trend) [moh-uganda-ahspr-2024]

**Top 20 conditions by primary care prevalence (Uganda, 209,734 visits, 2009–2010):**
Malaria (48.3%) | Cough/cold (19.4%) | Intestinal worms (6.6%) | GI disorders (5.5%) | Pneumonia (3.9%) | Diarrhea (3.9%) | Skin diseases (3.8%) | Urinary tract infection (3.4%) | Oral diseases (2.3%) | Injuries/trauma (2.2%) | Eye conditions (1.8%) | ENT conditions (1.8%) | Pelvic inflammatory disease (1.7%) | STIs (1.6%) | Anaemia (0.7%) | Hypertension (0.7%) | Typhoid fever (0.7%) | HIV/AIDS (0.6%) | Asthma (0.5%) | Arthritis (0.5%).

### Kenya and Tanzania Triangulation

**Kenya** (household surveys): Hypertension prevalence 24–29% (adults 18–69); only ~7% achieve control [kenya-hypertension-study]. Major inpatient diagnoses: pneumonia, malaria, gastrointestinal infections, meningitis, injuries [kapsowar-kenya-study].

**Tanzania** (health facility survey): Chronic diseases account for 58% of hospital outpatient visits vs. 20% at HC III and 13% at dispensary level. Hypertension and diabetes are primary care focus at hospital level [tanzania-ncd-readiness-study].

### Key Epidemiological Drivers Across Cohort

1. **Malaria burden dominance**: Uganda has the highest incidence globally (478 cases/1,000/year) [who-uganda-country-brief]. Plasmodium falciparum is primary species.
2. **Maternal-neonatal disease**: Maternal mortality ratio 336/100,000 (2016 DHS); neonatal mortality 19/1,000 [uganda-dhs-2016].
3. **TB co-epidemiology**: Tuberculosis prevalence 200/100,000; treatment success 72% (below WHO 85% target) [moh-uganda-ahspr-2024].
4. **HIV/AIDS**: Estimated 1.2 million people living with HIV in Uganda; prevalence 6.2% (ages 15–49) [worldbank-hiv-uganda].
5. **Rising NCD burden**: NCDs responsible for 36% of all-cause mortality by 2019; age-standardized mortality rate for CVD/CRD/Cancer/Diabetes: 709/100,000 (males), 506/100,000 (females) [moh-uganda-ncd-profile-2023].
6. **Mental health surge**: Cases rose from 455,153 (2021/22) to 666,239 (2024/25); 57.4% female [moh-uganda-ahspr-2024].
7. **Nutrition and anaemia**: Child anaemia 53% (ages 6–59 months); women of reproductive age 32% [uganda-dhs-2016].
