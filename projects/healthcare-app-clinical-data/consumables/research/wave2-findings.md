## Discrepancies & Limitations

1. **Imaging (16 vs. +20 target):** EMHSLU Section B/C inventory less granular for imaging supplies; attempt to add additional imaging items (e.g., US probe covers mentioned in brief) revealed no further detail in source
2. **Pharmacy (19 vs. +25 target):** Syringe drivers, IV pumps, infusion-pump-specific tubing not enumerated separately in EMHSLU; consolidated as "Syringe driver consumables" kit
3. **Dental & Office (slightly under targets):** Sources provided broad categories; specialized dental endodontic files, ortho wires not extensively listed
4. **No invasive hemodynamics (Swan-Ganz, CVP lines, etc.):** EMHSLU specifies "Invasive blood pressure" generically; specific catheter variants (Swan-Ganz) not mentioned in source

## Recommendations for Phase 2 (QA/Validation)

1. **Code mapping:** Submit [GAP] gmdn_code and unspsc_code fields to external code authority (GMDN, UNSPSC) for systematic mapping
2. **Inventory cross-check:** Verify against physical RRH (Regional Referral Hospital) stock records in one pilot district (e.g., Fort Portal, Jinja) to confirm item availability
3. **Paediatric dosing/variants:** Segment all paeds items into separate rows (per book-derived recommendations) during Phase 2 data normalization
4. **Duplicate detection:** Run Levenshtein distance check on item_name + specification to catch near-duplicates (e.g. "Foley 12Fr vs. Foley, 2-way, 12Fr")
5. **LOINC/SNOMED mapping (Lab cohort):** Lab items (media, reagents, tubes) should carry LOINC observation codes in Phase 2 enrichment
