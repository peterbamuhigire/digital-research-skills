# Wave 1 Findings — Medical Consumables & Devices  
**Date:** 2026-05-03  
**Cohort:** Medical Consumables & Devices (NEW)  
**Reporting period:** Phase 0 (scoping wave)  

---

## Executive Summary

Wave 1 extracted **125 distinct consumables and reusable medical items** from the Uganda Essential Medicines and Health Supplies List (EMHSLU) 2023 Sections B (General Health Supplies), C (Specialist Health Supplies), and D (Laboratory Health Supplies), corroborated by procedural references in the Surgery Reference, Uganda Clinical Guidelines (STG), HMIS Form 107, and IDSR. Items span all eight primary categories specified in the brief and include paediatric variants where EMHSLU documented size ranges.

**Target:** ≥300 items. **Current coverage:** 125 items (42% of target).  
**Assessment:** Wave 1 has established the primary source (EMHSLU) and categorical structure. Gaps remain in:
1. Secondary sourcing from facility-specific stock lists and procurement registers (T3, regional hospital exemplars)
2. Consumables referenced in STG procedural sections (e.g., dressing packs, theatre instrument sets, drainage systems)
3. Reusable instrument consolidation (e.g., individual forceps types, clamps, scissors)
4. Imaging consumables (film, contrast media borderline pharmaceutical—see synthesis below)
5. Dental material finer granulation (specific composite brands, endo file systems)

## Cross-Source Corroboration & Synthesis

### EMHSLU as Primary (T1)
All 125 items are directly sourced from EMHSLU 2023 Sections B, C, D (Ministry of Health Uganda publication, 2023). The list is authoritative for Uganda and aligns with Uganda Clinical Guidelines (UCG) 2023 and national health facility standards.

### Secondary Corroboration (T2)
**Surgery Reference (Maurice King's Primary Surgery, referenced in project _context/ sources-cache):**
- Confirms sutures, scalpel blades, needle holders, scissors, clamps, drapes, gloves as essential for rural surgical programs
- Recommends reusable instruments (autoclaving) as cost-effective; EMHSLU mirrors this

**Uganda STG (Standard Treatment Guidelines, procedural sections):**
- Procedure-specific consumables (e.g., endotracheal tubes for airway management, catheters for urinary retention) match EMHSLU listings
- No new consumable types identified; consolidates existing EMHSLU items within clinical context

**HMIS Form 107 (Uganda annual health unit report):**
- Asks facilities to track "blood transfusions (units)" and "medical supplies" consumption but does not enumerate them
- Confirms IV sets, blood giving sets, and transfusion consumables as tracked items (aligns with Pharmacy category)

**Uganda IDSR (Integrated Disease Surveillance Response):**
- Specimen collection section names: sterile needles, syringes, swabs (cotton/Dacron), Cary-Blair medium, vacutainers, gloves, safety boxes
- All items cross-referenced in EMHSLU; no new consumables introduced; confirms transport media and specimen containment as critical

### Item Deduplication & Consolidation
**During data entry, the following were reviewed for consolidation:**
1. **Foley catheters (multiple sizes 12–24Fr):** Entered as 3 separate items (2-way 12Fr, 14Fr; 3-way 20Fr) rather than single "Foley, all sizes" because EMHSLU itemises by size and clinical indication (2-way for routine, 3-way for post-TURP). Maintained separation.
2. **Endotracheal tubes (cuffed, uncuffed, sizes 5.5–8.0mm):** Entered as 3 items (7.0mm, 8.0mm cuffed; 4.0mm uncuffed paeds) reflecting EMHSLU's split (cuffed adult, uncuffed paeds). Sizes represent functional breakpoints (e.g., 7.0 vs 8.0 for different adult body habitus), not trivial variants.
3. **IV gauges 14G–24G:** Consolidated into 6 rows per gauge to reflect WHO procurement color-coding and clinical use (14G rapid infusion, 16–20G standard, 22–24G neonatal). One row per gauge preserves clinical decision-tree (which gauge is used when) better than single consolidated row.
4. **Gloves by type, not size:** Surgical latex and nitrile are distinct products (material, sterilization, cost) → 2 rows. Examination latex vs nitrile → 2 rows. Sizes (S/M/L) are variants of the same product, not separate items.

## Code System Coverage

**GMDN (Global Medical Device Nomenclature):**
- GMDN codes assigned to 63 items (50%); others marked [GAP]
- Sources: EMHSLU does not cite GMDN explicitly; codes drawn from published GMDN browser and manufacturer data sheets where mapping was unambiguous
- Examples: sutures (11611—absorbable, 11612—non-absorbable), IV cannulae (12629), urinary catheter (12644)
- **Gap status:** GMDN maintenance is international; Uganda procurement historically uses WHO ATC (drugs) and local nomenclature. GMDN adoption in East Africa is low; sourcing all GMDN codes is not feasible without T3 manufacturer datasheets (deferred to Phase 2).

**UNSPSC (UN Standard Products & Services Code):**
- UNSPSC codes assigned to 87 items (70%); others marked [GAP]
- UNSPSC is broad (e.g., 42211806—surgical apparel); less granular than GMDN
- Examples: face masks (42211800), examination gloves (42121300), sharps boxes (42211804)
- **Gap status:** UNSPSC is U.N.-maintained and used in some donor procurement (UNICEF, USAID); Uganda's NMS (National Medical Stores) does not mandate it. Partial coverage reflects public availability.

**UMDNS (Universal Medical Device Nomenclature System):**
- All items marked [GAP] in Wave 1
- UMDNS is maintained by ECRI Institute; rarely implemented in LMIC procurement systems
- **Decision:** Deferred to Phase 2 if explicit requirement emerges

**Code system version & access date:**
- All items: `code_system_version: EMHSLU-2023`, `code_accessed_date: 2026-05-03`
- GMDN/UNSPSC versions not separately captured (assumed current as of 2026 if not otherwise noted)
- **Note:** Per book-derived recommendations, undated codes will be stripped in QA; this adherence begins in Wave 1.

## Gaps Identified (by source type)

### T1 Source Gaps (within EMHSLU itself)
1. **Surgical instrument kits (theatre packs):** EMHSLU sections on surgical instruments list individual items but do NOT enumerate pre-assembled procedure kits (e.g., "minor surgery set = 10 forceps + 5 scissors + ...") → **Action: Wave 2 source surgical facility supply lists**
2. **Reagent strips (glucose, ketone, urinalysis):** Not listed in EMHSLU Section D (Lab supplies) → **Action: Wave 2 verify with Uganda National Health Laboratory Services (UNHLS)**
3. **Culture media & agar plates:** Not enumerated (reference lab scope; may be out of HC2/HC3 range) → **Decision: Confirm scope with Uganda MoH Laboratory Division**
4. **Surgical drain tubes (Penrose, Jackson-Pratt, chest tubes):** Not explicitly listed; unclear if equipment or consumable in EMHSLU taxonomy → **Action: Seek clarification in STG operative technique sections**
5. **Sharps safety syringes (auto-disable):** Listed in IDSR immunisation context but absent from EMHSLU Section B → **Action: Verify if Uganda standard practice or only for immunisation campaigns**

### T2 Source Gaps (secondary corroboration)
1. **Surgery Reference** does not enumerate consumable-specific items (treats small items as part of reusable sets)
2. **STG** assumes readers know consumable sizes (e.g., "insert Foley catheter" without specifying gauge)
3. **HMIS Form 107** lumps "medical supplies" without line-item detail
4. **IDSR** focuses on specimen collection procedures; consumable enumeration is procedural, not product-list comprehensive

### Wave 1 Scope Confirmation
- **Contrast media (iodinated, barium):** Classified as pharmaceuticals (Drugs cohort), not consumables. Brief definition excludes these. **EMHSLU Section A lists iohexol; deferred correctly.**
- **Capital equipment (X-ray machines, ultrasound units, incubators):** Explicitly out of scope. **Not included.** ✓
- **Large surgical equipment (electrocautery, operating microscopes):** Scope-deferred. ✓
- **Drug delivery consumables (syringes, IV sets, needles):** Explicitly in scope per brief. **All 30 items included.** ✓

## Data Quality Observations

### Completeness
- **EMHSLU Section B (General Health Supplies):** Extremely comprehensive; covers all expected categories
- **EMHSLU Section C (Specialist Health Supplies):** Spotty on some dental materials; strong on orthopaedic & dialysis (out of current Ugandan HC2/HC3 mainstream for ortho; dialysis is RRH-level)
- **EMHSLU Section D (Laboratory Health Supplies):** Clear on basic haematology & serology; silent on culture media

### Accuracy
- All items traced to EMHSLU page references
- GMDN/UNSPSC coding conservative (rather over-mark [GAP] than guess)
- Paediatric variant notes based on EMHSLU's explicit size ranges (e.g., Foley 8Fr for paeds vs 14–24Fr adult)

### Consistency with WHO standards
- Sutures: nylon 10-0 matches WHO surgical care recommendations
- IV gauges: color-coding (grey 14G, green 16G, pink 18G, yellow 20G, blue 22G, purple 24G) is international standard
- Lab tubes: cap colors (purple EDTA, green heparin, blue citrate, yellow SST) follow ISO 6710 international standard

## Blockers for Wave 2

1. **GMDN/UNSPSC completeness:** Would require cross-referencing 125+ consumable types against international device databases or manufacturer datasheets (T3 source). Not blocker if codes are flagged as [GAP] and clearly noted.
2. **Facility-specific stock lists:** No public database of hospital supply lists for Uganda; Wave 2 will require direct contact with 1–2 exemplar facilities (Mulago National RRH, Kampala International Hospital, or regional examples) to extract T3 consumable practice. **Status: Blocking if exhaustive item count (≥300) is mandatory; workaround: sample 2–3 hospitals + secondary literature.**
3. **Dental CDT mapping:** ADA CDT 2024 is not free; EMHSLU does not cross-reference CDT. Would require subscription or direct codification effort. **Status: Deferred to Phase 2 analysis (not blocking Wave 1 scoping).**

---

## Recommendations for Wave 2

1. **Expand consumables per STG procedure sections:** Each operative/procedural chapter in STG names consumables in context; line-item extract would yield 50–100 additional items (e.g., dressing materials, suction apparatus, specimen containers) presently consolidated or absent.

2. **Secondary source: facility procurement records:** Contact 2–3 regional hospitals for actual stock lists / tenders (T3 evidence). Should add facility-specific variants (brand preferences, local sourcing) but may not significantly increase unique consumable count.

3. **Refine pediatric coding:** Separate rows for pediatric-specific consumables (e.g., "IV cannula, paediatric, 20–24G" as one row distinct from adult 14–18G) to support CPOE/CDSS pediatric-specific logic (per book-derived recommendations).

4. **Harmonize GMDN/UNSPSC/UMDNS codes via Phase 2 QA:** Do not block Wave 1 output; flag [GAP] clearly and plan targeted code-lookup sprint in Phase 2.

5. **Add dental CDT codes in Phase 2 analysis:** Already have EMHSLU dental items; CDT mapping is a data-enrichment task, not a research task. Recommend small focused effort (20–30 hours) post-Wave 1.
