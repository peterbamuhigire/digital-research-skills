## Blockers & Limitations

1. **EMHSLU OCR artifacts:** Original EMHSLU was PDF (Ministry of Health Uganda, September 2023 edition). Text extracted via OCR and markdown conversion. ~2–3% of cell values likely corrupted (especially multi-line cells, special characters). Examples:
   - "Bupivacaine (with" — incomplete text (parenthesis likely spans row break)
   - "Artovastatin" — probable OCR error for "Atorvastatin" (corrected in some rows)
   - "Bendroflume-" — truncated (likely "Bendroflumethiazide")

2. **ATC code inference without explicit mapping:** EMHSLU does not provide ATC codes. Mapping requires WHO ATC/DDD Index lookup by INN. Risk: ~1–2% of obscure INNs (combination products, regional names) may not have exact WHO ATC match.

3. **No direct register access:** Uganda NDA, Kenya PPB, Tanzania TMDA registers (Tier-1 authorities) not accessible via automated web scraping in this wave. Registration status flagged [unverified]; Phase 2 QA must manually verify.

4. **Pediatric dosing largely absent from EMHSLU:** This cohort is adult-formulary focused. Pediatric dose summaries drawn from WHO guidelines and standard references (e.g., WHO Model Formulary); any discrepancy with Uganda Clinical Guidelines flagged [GAP].

## Recommendations for Phase 2 QA

1. **Spot-check ATC codes** for 10% of rows (n=5) against WHO ATC/DDD Index:
   - Verify INN spelling vs. WHO INN standard
   - Confirm ATC level-5 codes (last 2 digits) match WHO reference
   - Flag any [atc-emhslu-mapping] notes for human review

2. **Validate EMHSLU OCR:** Re-read original EMHSLU PDF for 5 problematic rows (e.g., "Bendroflume-", "Bupivacaine (with") to confirm/correct values

3. **Register verification (NDA Uganda):** Web scrape or manual check for top 20 drugs by dispensing volume (estimated: amoxicillin, paracetamol, diclofenac, artemisinin combinations, antiretrovirals) to populate `registered_uganda_nda`

4. **Pediatric dosing triage:** Flag rows with [GAP] on `paeds_dose_summary` for review against Uganda Clinical Guidelines (UCG), especially:
   - Anti-TB drugs (isoniazid, ethambutol) — requires weight-based rounding protocols
   - Antiretrovirals (lamivudine) — already on UCG Section 6 (HIV), align with national ART formularies
   - Corticosteroids — context-dependent dosing (inflammation vs. endocrine vs. emergency); UCG sections vary

5. **Cross-reference WHO EML:** All Wave 4 drugs should be checked against WHO Model List 23rd edition (2023). Those NOT on EML (estimated ~30%) should be flagged with clinical justification (e.g., "on EMHSLU but not WHO EML; regional burden of disease driver" or "second-line agent retained locally").
