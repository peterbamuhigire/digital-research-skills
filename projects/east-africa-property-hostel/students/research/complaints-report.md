# East African University Student Hostel Complaints — Comprehensive Report

Compiled 2026-04-25 from 50+ public sources (news media, social platforms, blogs, university reports, academic research).

## Executive summary

Student hostel complaints across East African universities split cleanly into two categories: **difficulty finding accommodation** and **poor living conditions once housed**. Complaints span Uganda (Makerere, Kyambogo, MUBS), Kenya (University of Nairobi, Kenyatta, Strathmore), Tanzania (UDSM, Mzumbe), Rwanda (University of Rwanda), and Burundi. Recurring issues: scams targeting freshers, severe capacity shortages, unfair eviction practices, sanitation failures, security threats, rent hikes, and missing utilities.

---

## Part 1 — Finding a hostel

### 1.1 Scams & fraud targeting freshers

**Makerere University, Uganda.** A fresher lost **UGX 2 million** (their tuition) to a conman who promised to "double their money." Campus police log at least **2 fraud cases per week** targeting new students. Method: ex-students befriend freshers, offer help or jobs, then steal.
Source: [Independent Uganda](https://www.independent.co.ug/makerere-university-fresher-loses-sh2m-to-conman/)

**Kenya — fake-listing scams.** Common patterns:
- Scammers pose as property owners, collect holding deposits, vanish.
- Fraudsters rent a real property, advertise to multiple students, collect deposits + first month's rent, then refuse entry (keys don't work, fraudster uncontactable).
- Photo theft from legitimate sites makes fakes appear authentic.

**Red flags students name:** prices below market, brokers demanding deposits before viewings, owners refusing phone/video, rushed commit pressure.
Sources: [IDP](https://www.idp.com/kenya/blog/tips-avoid-scams-booking-overseas-accommodation/), [Gradding Homes](https://www.graddinghomes.com/blog/guide/student-accommodation-scams), [Expat.com Kenya](https://expat.com/forum/viewtopic.php?id=845667)

### 1.2 Online booking platforms — early & uneven

Emerging platforms with limited regional penetration:
- **Jiunge** (Kenya) — verified institutions and hostels, "no middlemen"
- **HostelFinder** (Kenya) — student accommodation discovery
- **Katalyma** (Ghana / East Africa) — escrow, verified listings, video tours
- **StayHub GH** — verified agents, secure payments
- **Hostelconnect** (Africa-wide, founded 2016)

No widespread mentions of "Roost," "Acomodeasy," or hostel-specific apps for Makerere/UoN — suggesting either limited adoption or rebranding. Most students still rely on word-of-mouth, bulletin boards, and brokers.

### 1.3 Capacity crisis

| University | Demand | Supply | Coverage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kyambogo (Uganda) | 8,000 applications | 1,400 beds in 5 halls | ~17% |
| Kenyatta U (Kenya) | 74,000 undergrads | 10,000 beds in 21 hostels | ~13% |
| University of Nairobi | — | ~40,000 beds across all campuses | ~25% |
| UR Huye (Rwanda, 2025) | 6,000 newcomers | ~3,000 beds | ~50% |
| UDSM (Tanzania) | 20,000–25,000 admitted/year | ~9,982 beds | ~40% |
| Mzumbe Mbeya (Tanzania) | ~3,500 students | ~500 on-campus rooms | ~14% |

**Rwanda 2025 spike.** UR Huye students slept in the **university gymnasium** when newcomers exceeded capacity. Private hostels now cram **up to 4 girls per room** designed for 2. Nearest available accommodation is **1 hour from campus**.
Source: [New Times Rwanda](https://www.newtimes.co.rw/article/30931/news/education/ur-huye-campus-grapples-with-student-accommodation-as-academic-year-starts/)

**Kenya overspill.** Over 22,000 Kenyatta U students live off-campus in Kahawa Sukari, Roysambu, Kahawa Wendani — areas with documented insecurity.

Sources: [Makerere Hostel Inspection Report 2023](https://www.mak.ac.ug/sites/default/files/2024-03/Hostel-Inspection-Report-2023.pdf), [The Star Kenya](https://www.the-star.co.ke/news/2026-03-27-my-struggle-with-water-shortages-at-university-of-nairobi-hostels), [Mzumbe University](https://mcc.mzumbe.ac.tz/student/dean-of-students/off-campus-hostels)

### 1.4 Broker exploitation

Brokers in Kenya and Uganda commonly:
- Demand deposits before showing properties
- Rush students to commit
- Charge 5–15% of first month's rent in commission
- Steer students to landlords who pay kickbacks rather than to better-fit options

## Part 2 — Living in a hostel

### 2.1 Sanitation, water, power

**Makerere Pearl View Hostel (Uganda).**
> "The sanitation at the hostel is very poor. Sewage is everywhere. It's worse in the compound, near the reading rooms and the canteen."

Broken sewage, toilet, and water pipes; awful smells preventing sleep; management unresponsive.
Source: [Dorothy Nakamyuka blog](https://dorothynakamyuka.wordpress.com/2014/03/14/makerere-pearl-view-hostel-sudents-decry-poor-sanitation/)

**University of Nairobi (Jan–Mar 2026).** Ongoing water shortages since the start of second semester.
> "Water shortages have turned hostel life into a daily struggle, reshaping the university experience into one of endurance rather than learning."

Available water is sometimes dirty; students buy bottled water out-of-pocket.
Source: [The Star Kenya](https://www.the-star.co.ke/news/2026-03-27-my-struggle-with-water-shortages-at-university-of-nairobi-hostels)

**Uganda infrastructure context.** Only 42–45% of Ugandans have electricity access; Kampala water shortages tied to vandalism shutting down Katosi Water Works. Students report power cuts mid-class wasting time.

### 2.2 Bedbugs & overcrowding

**Tanzania research.** A study of 384 Moshi boarding-school students found **58.1% bedbug infestation**; **72.6% of infested students** lived in overcrowded dormitories. Only 32.7% knew bedbug biology and prevention.
Source: [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/343623835_Prevalence_of_Bedbugs_infestation_Crowding_and_Awareness_level_among_Government_boarding_Secondary_school_students_in_Moshi_urban_Tanzania)

**Makerere — Joshua Arinda's account.**
> "After barely a month in residence I began to feel painful itchy bites at night... Even when I sprayed, it didn't help because people kept bringing them back. The infestation spread amongst rooms and visitors. This discomfort exposed deeper issues — poor hygiene, overcrowding, and weak management."

He left the hostel.
Source: [Campus Bee](https://campusbee.ug/lifestyle/hostels/hostel-review-list-and-costs-mak-hostels/)

### 2.3 Overcrowding & noise

**Kyambogo named hostels:**
- **Paradise** — multiple deckers, cramped
- **Madonna** — lacks facilities; inconsistent shuttles
- **Bavana** — overcrowded, inadequate water
> "Some hostels have added extra beds and congested rooms, exposing first-year students to manipulation from those above them."

Source: [Campus Bee](https://campusbee.ug/lifestyle/truth-is-bitter-an-unbiased-review-of-hostels-at-kyambogo-university/)

**UoN Chiromo TikTok reaction.** Students paying KSh 30–40K per semester at one of Kenya's "World Class Universities":
> "I was shocked, honey, I was in tears almost! All love no shade but they can be better."

Source: [@kenyabytes TikTok](https://www.tiktok.com/@kenyabytes/video/7416418181759110405)

### 2.4 Security & theft

- Burglaries inside Makerere-area hostels documented in Daily Monitor.
- Halls at Kyambogo (Nanziri, Mandela, Pearl) have no entry restrictions — strangers walk in.
- UoN private stock described as "old and rundown buildings lacking ample security."
- Kenyan senator: skyrocketing fees are "forcing students into slums," compounding security risk.
- Rwanda — private hostels lack security infrastructure; female students face heightened risk.

Sources: [Monitor](https://www.monitor.co.ug/uganda/special-reports/how-safe-are-university-students-residing-in-hostels--1483070), [Nation Kenya](https://nation.africa/kenya/news/Hostels-crisis-death-trap-college-students-/1950946-5027630-item-1-dsckpf/index.html), [Kenyans.co.ke](https://www.kenyans.co.ke/news/114523-skyrocketing-hostel-fees-forcing-students-slums-senator-sifuna)

### 2.5 Sexual harassment & caretaker abuse

**Olympia Hostel, Kikoni (Makerere area).** Caretaker **Reuben Nahabwe suspended for alleged sexual harassment** of female residents (Aug 2019) after a student-leader ultimatum. Independent investigation committee formed with civil society, gender, and student representatives.
Source: [Independent Uganda](https://www.independent.co.ug/the-management-of-olympia-hostel-in-kikoni-a-kampala-suburb-has-succumbed-to-pressure-and-suspended-its-caretaker-reuben-nahabwe-for-alleged-sexual-harassment-dr-joseph-byamugisha-the-managin/)

This is the only publicly documented case — the underreporting is itself a finding.

**Kenya tenant law.** Landlords/caretakers cannot harass tenants or enter without permission. Reported incidents include caretakers evicting without proper notice. Recourse: Rent Tribunal.

### 2.6 Unfair eviction & double-letting

**Cotilde Hostel, Kikumi (Makerere area, May 2021).** Threatened to evict students **during exams**. Some had paid in full but management re-sold rooms at higher prices. Reversed only after Campus Bee exposed it.
Sources: [Campus Bee exposé](https://campusbee.ug/lifestyle/hostels/exposed-makerere-hostel-kicking-out-students-unfairly-revealed/), [Campus Bee follow-up](https://campusbee.ug/lifestyle/hostels/cotilde-hostel-reverses-order-to-vacate-students-corrects-earlier-notice/)

### 2.7 Deposit theft

**Uganda — staggering scale.**
> "80% of tenants have never gotten a refund of their security deposit from their previous landlords even when the house has been left in good condition."

Common landlord excuses: invented damages, "unauthorized renovations," vague maintenance charges, simply disappearing.

Legal framework (Landlord & Tenant Act, 2022): max one month's rent, refund within 30 days with itemized deductions. Widely ignored.
Sources: [Monitor](https://www.monitor.co.ug/uganda/magazines/homes-and-property/is-the-security-deposit-nothing-but-a-big-scam-for-landlords--4324306), [est8Ledger](https://www.est8ledger.com/index.php/blog/boosting-tenant-confidence-uganda-security-deposit)

### 2.8 Rent hikes

**University of Nairobi.** Sh6,000/semester (2021) → Sh43,000/semester (2024) = ~617% in 3 years. After Sept 2024 strike threatening CBD shutdown, halved to KSh 21,500 (single) / KSh 15,500 (double).
Sources: [Standard Kenya](https://www.standardmedia.co.ke/education/article/2001526250/uon-caves-to-student-protests-cuts-hostel-charges-by-50pc), [Kenya Times](https://thekenyatimes.com/latest-kenya-times-news/uon-reduces-hostel-fees/)

**Uganda 2023.** Hostels around Makerere, Kyambogo, MUBS raised fees by **UGX 50,000–150,000** between intakes. MUBS no longer has any halls for government-sponsored students — all compete for private hostels.
Sources: [Independent Uganda](https://www.independent.co.ug/makerere-students-find-solace-in-rentals-due-to-hiked-hostel-fees/), [Monitor](https://www.monitor.co.ug/uganda/news/national/university-hostels-increase-fees-ahead-of-first-semester-1839624)

### 2.9 Internet & connectivity

Most Uganda hostels run **one connection for the whole building** — dozens to hundreds of simultaneous users on cheap routers. Slow speeds, dead zones, downtime during power outages. Students miss online classes and assignments.
Sources: [Savanna Fibre](https://savannafibre.com/2025/01/09/common-internet-challenges-in-uganda-and-how-to-overcome-them/), [Quora thread](https://www.quora.com/How-do-I-get-uninterrupted-Internet-connectivity-in-Uganda)

### 2.10 Hidden costs

Beyond quoted rent, Makerere-area students typically pay extra for: security, WiFi (despite "free WiFi" ads), furniture/maintenance, cleaning/laundry (sometimes mandatory), electricity tokens, water. Monthly outflow exceeds advertised rent.
Source: [Campus Bee guide](https://campusbee.ug/lifestyle/hostels/guide-on-hostel-options-and-costs-what-every-university-student-should-know/)

## Part 3 — Systemic causes

1. **Government underinvestment.** Public funding of higher ed has steadily decreased; universities prioritise teaching/research over hostels.
2. **Enrollment outpaced housing.** Kenya: 27,000 (1990) → 550,000 (2019). Hostels did not scale.
3. **Unregulated private market.** Universities offload housing to private operators with minimal oversight or enforced standards.
4. **No standardised landlord/caretaker code of conduct.** Sexual harassment, unfair eviction, deposit theft persist because consequences are rare.

Sources: [University World News](https://www.universityworldnews.com/post.php?story=20230815124304), [CrossBoundary Real Estate](https://crossboundary.com/changes-in-student-housing-models-across-africa/)

---

## Part 4 — Institutional responses

- **Makerere (2023)** — Hostel Inspection Committee visited 37 hostels across Katanga, Kikoni, Kagugube, Kitamanyaga, Kikumi. Began accrediting hostels.
- **UoN (2025/26)** — Halved fees after strike; approved 4,000-bed PPP hostel construction.
- **UR Huye (2025)** — Mobilising Private Sector Federation; targeting 60% undergrad coverage in 5 years.
- **Kenyatta U** — PPP with Africa Integras for 10,000-capacity Kahawa hostel (signed 2015).

---

## Conclusion

The crisis is **dual** (access + quality) and **systemic** (capacity, regulation, accountability). University inspections and PPP hostels move slowly; private platforms (Jiunge, Katalyma) move faster but have thin coverage. Most vulnerable: first-years, female students, low-income students forced to choose between food/books and decent housing.

Source: [IWACU](https://www.iwacu-burundi.org/englishnews/26-final-year-students-at-cnrsp-expelled-from-dormitories/)

## P2.2 Mental health, dropout, and academic outcomes

- **Makerere undergrad mental health study** — depression prevalence 16.2% (BDI score 10+) general student population vs 4.0% in Faculty of Medicine. Housing was not isolated as a variable but provides a baseline.
Source: [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16757997/), [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1472270/)

- **Morogoro 3-university housing study (Tanzania, n=104).** Students living near campus performed significantly better. Cleanliness, group satisfaction, facility quality strongly predicted academic performance. Higher rent **negatively** affected performance.
Source: [CrossBoundary](https://crossboundary.com/student-housing-learning-outcomes-covid/)

- **Depression × dropout (South African undergrad cohort).** Severe mental distress increased dropout likelihood **7-fold**. Translates the housing-stress-to-dropout pathway across SSA.
Source: [BMC Public Health](https://bmcpublichealth.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s12889-022-14517-7)

- **Makerere dissertation — Prince Hostel, Kikoni.** "Analysis of the effect of increased hostel fees on university students' welfare" — direct primary-source academic work tying fee hikes to student welfare.
Source: [Makerere Dissertations](https://dissertations.mak.ac.ug/handle/20.500.12281/11754)

## P2.5 Female-specific harm — extended

- **Olympia Hostel (Kikoni).** Custodian Reuben Nahabwe suspended in 2019 over allegations of "feasting on female students." Online resident petition forced action.
Source: [PML Daily](https://www.pmldaily.com/news/2019/08/makerere-top-hostel-olympia-suspends-custodian-over-sexual-harassment.html)

- **Transactional sex / "sponsor" dynamics.** SSA-wide academic literature documents lecturer-student and authority-student transactional sex with power imbalances and economic coercion. Hostel housing instability is a force multiplier on this dynamic.
Sources: [PubMed (Benin)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26808397/), [Springer Nature](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s43545-023-00611-z), [PLOS One](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0260886)

- **Period poverty.** Uganda: ~28% absence on period days vs 7% on non-period days; ~25% of 12–18-year-old girls drop out when menstruation begins. Hostels with poor water access and no product provision worsen this.
Sources: [UN Women Africa](https://africa.unwomen.org/en/stories/news/2024/05/period-poverty-why-millions-of-girls-and-women-cannot-afford-their-periods), [Ballard Brief](https://ballardbrief.byu.edu/issue-briefs/lack-of-menstrual-hygiene-management-among-women-and-girls-in-east-africa)

- **Pregnancy & education.** Tanzania reversed its pregnant-student schooling ban in 2021 (Education Circular No. 2). Estimated 6,550–15,000 girls/year had been pushed out previously. No specific hostel-eviction-on-pregnancy cases indexed yet — but the policy risk frame exists.
Sources: [Center for Reproductive Rights](https://reproductiverights.org/news/tanzanias-pregnant-schoolgirls-education/), [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2021/10/06/tanzania-pregnant-student-ban-harms-thousands)

## P2.9 Bed-space economy — detail

- **Pricing structure (Kampala):** Traditional hostels UGX 500k–2m/semester; rental houses UGX 150k–500k/month; **bed-spaces priced below both** because tenants share utility costs.
- **Geography:** Wandegeya, Kikoni, Kikumi, Kasubi (Makerere); Roysambu, Kahawa Sukari (Nairobi); Mwenge, Ubungo (Dar).
- **Household management:** A senior student or non-student "household head" sub-lets and manages utility splits — outside any regulatory frame.
- **Safety implication:** Female safety in mixed-gender bed-space households is plausibly worse than in formal hostels, but undocumented.
Sources: [Monitor](https://www.monitor.co.ug/uganda/magazines/homes-and-property/bed-spaces-cost-effective-short-term-accommodation-5004932), [Nymy Net](https://nymynet.com/what-is-bedspace-a-cheaper-rent-option-for-kampala-workers/)

## P2.10 Refugee students

- **Kenya:** 426,822 registered refugees / asylum seekers (Feb 2025). Somali (54%), South Sudanese (24.5%) majority. Kakuma students pass national exams at 88% vs 76% national average.

## P2.11 USIU & Strathmore — explicit risk-transfer model

- **USIU-Africa (Kenya).** Explicitly does NOT manage off-campus hostels. "[Does] not accept any responsibility or liability for the choices made."
Source: [USIU Housing Department](https://www.usiu.ac.ke/246/housing-department/)

- **Strathmore University (Kenya).** No on-campus accommodation. Students use private hostels (KSh 15,000–24,000/month). Keri Residence (female-focused, catered) one identified option.

This is a **distinct accommodation model**: institutional disclaimer + 100% private market. Risk concentrates on students.

## P2.12 Court records — gap confirmed

- Kenya Rent Restriction Tribunal jurisdiction is mapped: ≤ KSh 2,500 standard rent, residential tenancies (excl. shops/hotels). Cap 296.
- Civil Case 56 of 2015 ([Kenya Law](http://kenyalaw.org/caselaw/cases/view/114054)) determined the Tribunal lacks jurisdiction over house tenancies.
- **No indexed student-hostel-specific case law.** The Tribunal framework exists; students don't use it.
- Uganda Magistrate Court records: not indexed online; gap remains.
