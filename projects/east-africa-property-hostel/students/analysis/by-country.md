# Country-by-country breakdown

## Uganda

**Universities covered:** Makerere, Kyambogo, MUBS

**Capacity:** Kyambogo 1,400 beds for 8,000 applicants (~17%). MUBS has **zero** halls for government-sponsored students — 100% in private market.

**Dominant complaints:**
- Fresher scams (Sh2m doubler case; 2 cases/week at Makerere)
- Sanitation collapse (Pearl View)
- Bedbugs (Arinda; widespread)
- Sexual harassment (Olympia caretaker case)
- Unfair eviction & double-letting (Cotilde)
- Deposit theft (80% non-refund — the most striking single statistic in this research)
- Rent hikes Sh50–150k between intakes (2023)
- Power & water infrastructure failures
- Hidden fees (security, WiFi, electricity tokens)

**Institutional response:** Makerere 2023 Hostel Inspection Committee — 37 hostels visited, accreditation framework launched. Slow rollout; enforcement unclear.

**Named hostels:**
- Pearl View — sanitation failure
- Cotilde (Kikumi) — unfair eviction
- Olympia (Kikoni) — caretaker harassment, billing disputes
- Paradise / Madonna / Bavana (Kyambogo) — overcrowding, facility gaps
- New Nana — relatively positive reputation

## Kenya

**Universities covered:** University of Nairobi, Kenyatta U, Strathmore (no own hostels)

**Capacity:** ~25% national average across public unis. KU 10,000 beds for 74,000 students (~13%). Over 22,000 KU students in off-campus areas with documented insecurity.

**Dominant complaints:**
- Capacity shortage at scale
- Fake-listing & broker scams
- Water shortages (UoN Jan–Mar 2026 ongoing)
- Old, rundown stock with weak security
- Rent hikes (UoN 617% in 3 years before strike)
- Off-campus security risks pushing students into slums

**Institutional response:** UoN halved fees after Sept 2024 student strike threatening CBD shutdown. PPP for 4,000-bed construction. KU Africa Integras PPP for 10,000-bed Kahawa hostel signed 2015 — implementation status unclear.

**Notable:** Senator Sifuna publicly called out the slum-pushing dynamic. Strikes work. The Rent Tribunal exists but is not commonly used by students.

## Tanzania

**Universities covered:** UDSM, Mzumbe (Mbeya campus)

**Capacity:** UDSM ~9,982 beds for 20,000–25,000 admitted/year (~40%). Mzumbe Mbeya ~500 rooms for 3,500 students (~14%).

**Dominant complaints:**
- Severe shortage; off-campus rents Sh150–300k/semester
- Bedbug crisis (academic study: 58.1% infestation among Moshi boarding students; 72.6% of infested cases in overcrowded dorms)
- Female safety in off-campus housing — Mzumbe proposed 1,200-bed female hostel in response

**Institutional response:** Limited public reporting. Mzumbe Dean of Students publishes off-campus listings.

**Gap:** Less social-media complaint volume surfaces than Uganda/Kenya — possibly underreporting rather than absence of problems.

## Rwanda

**Universities covered:** University of Rwanda (Huye campus)

**Capacity 2025:** 6,000 newcomers, ~3,000 beds (~50% deficit).

**Dominant complaints:**
- Students sleeping in university gymnasium
- Private hostels 1 hour from campus
- 4 girls per room in 2-person rooms
- Inflated rents, few vacancies
- Female safety risk in off-campus stock with weak security infrastructure

**Institutional response:** Local government mobilising Private Sector Federation; 5-year target of 60% undergraduate coverage.

## Burundi & South Sudan

**South Sudan — University of Juba, June 2024.** South Sudan National Police raided an all-female hostel and arrested 23 students protesting a fee increase. ~16 students injured. The most severe documented police-on-student-housing incident in the regional corpus.

**Burundi — CNRSP Bujumbura, June 2024.** 26 final-year disabled students expelled from dormitories by police after protesting a decision sending them home. Quote: *"I don't have bus fare to go back to Muyinga province where I came from. And it is the same situation for many of my classmates."*
Source: [IWACU](https://www.iwacu-burundi.org/englishnews/26-final-year-students-at-cnrsp-expelled-from-dormitories/)

**University of Burundi.** Persistent housing shortage; on-campus capacity prioritised for rural students; supply has not kept up with enrolment.

Both countries are no longer coverage blanks but remain under-indexed internationally — Eye Radio (South Sudan) and IWACU (Burundi) carry the only consistent local journalism on hostel issues.

## Cross-country pattern

The **same five problems** appear in every country with sufficient data:
1. Capacity shortage (universal)
2. Rent hikes (universal)
3. Security/safety gaps (universal)
4. Sanitation/utilities failures (universal)
5. Weak management accountability — sexual harassment, unfair eviction, deposit theft (documented in UG, legally framed in KE, implied elsewhere)

The institutional responses are also similar: slow PPP construction, post-protest fee cuts, voluntary inspection schemes. **No country has a working private-hostel regulatory regime.**
