# Theme taxonomy — finding vs living

Two top-level categories, each with sub-themes. Each sub-theme tagged with **prevalence** (how widespread across countries/sources) and **severity** (impact on students).

## A. Finding a hostel

| Sub-theme | Prevalence | Severity | Key evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| A1. Fresher-targeted scams (fake doublers, fake landlords) | High (UG, KE) | High — full tuition can be lost | Sh2m Makerere case; KE fake-listing patterns |
| A2. Capacity shortage | Universal | High — students sleep in gyms | Kyambogo 17%, KU 13%, UR 50% deficit |
| A3. Broker exploitation (5–15% commissions, kickbacks) | High (UG, KE) | Medium | Field reports; no published rate cards |
| A4. No transparent online listings | Universal | Medium — drives reliance on brokers | Limited adoption of Jiunge/Katalyma |
| A5. Last-minute scrambles each intake | Universal | Medium | UR 2025 gymnasium incident; Mzumbe overflow |
| A6. Distance-from-campus tradeoff | High (KE, RW) | Medium — security risk in slums | KU 22,000 students in Kahawa Sukari/Roysambu |

## Cross-cutting risk multipliers

- **Female students** carry extra weight on B5, B6, B7, A6.
- **First-years** carry extra weight on A1, A2 (last to be allocated), B5 (manipulated by seniors).
- **Government-sponsored / loan-dependent students** carry extra weight on A2, B10 (loan delays + rent hikes compound).
- **MUBS-type universities** with no halls at all (Uganda) push 100% of students into the private market.

## What's not yet mapped

- Mental health impact of cumulative B-themes (anxiety, depression, dropout)
- Academic performance correlation
- Family financial strain (parents covering scam losses, deposit theft, rent hikes)
- Disability accessibility — zero coverage in current sources
