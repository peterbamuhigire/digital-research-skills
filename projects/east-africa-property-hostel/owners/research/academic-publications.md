# Academic & institutional publications — owner side

The peer-reviewed and grey-literature subset of the source list, formatted for citation. Use for grant applications, pitch decks, and academic writing.

## Primary institutional reports

**Makerere University.** *Hostel Inspection Report 2023.* Kampala: Makerere University, 2024. PDF. The first formal university hostel inspection programme in East Africa. Inspected 37 hostels in Katanga, Kikoni, Kagugube, Kitamanyaga, Kikume between May–July 2023. Established A/B/C grading and proposed an MOU framework between the university and hostel owners.
[mak.ac.ug](https://www.mak.ac.ug/sites/default/files/2024-03/Hostel-Inspection-Report-2023.pdf)

**Kenya National Bureau of Statistics.** *2023/24 Kenya Housing Survey Basic Report.* Nairobi: KNBS, 2025. PDF. National housing stock and rental market data.
[knbs.or.ke](https://www.knbs.or.ke/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/2023-24-Kenya-Housing-Survey-Basic-Report.pdf)

**World Bank.** *Stocktaking of the Housing Sector in Sub-Saharan Africa: Summary Report.* Washington DC: World Bank, undated. Documents 25%+ mortgage rates and capital scarcity for African housing development.
[worldbank.org](https://www.worldbank.org/content/dam/Worldbank/document/Africa/Report/stocktaking-of-the-housing-sector-in-sub-saharan-africa-summary-report.pdf)

**International Finance Corporation.** *Market Assessment: The Student Housing Landscape in South Africa.* Washington DC: IFC, 2021. The most rigorous PBSA market assessment for sub-Saharan Africa; benchmark for any East African equivalent that doesn't yet exist.
[ifc.org](https://www.ifc.org/en/insights-reports/2021/market-assessment-the-student-housing-landscape-in-south-africa)

## Peer-reviewed journal articles

**Revington, Nick.** "Financializing Through Crisis? Student Housing and Studentification During the Covid-19 Pandemic and Beyond." *Tijdschrift voor Economische en Sociale Geografie* 114, no. 4 (2023): 320–334.
- Documents the structural risks of PBSA's reliance on student mobility, exposed by COVID-19 disruption.
- [Wiley](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/tesg.12549)

**Mago, Stephen, et al.** "Off-campus housing supply and utilization: perspectives of landlords and students in Chinhoyi." *South African Geographical Journal* (2023).
- Closest African counterpart to a landlord-experience study; methodology transferable to East African research.
- [Taylor & Francis](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03736245.2023.2227150)

**Asare, Kwabena.** "Student Accommodation Selection Factors: The Perspective of Tertiary Students in Public Universities in Ghana." *Journal of African Real Estate Research* (2024).
- Demand-side framing relevant for Anglophone West Africa; useful for triangulating East African findings.
- [Taylor & Francis](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/09277544.2024.2426320)

**Huchzermeyer, Marie, et al.** "High rises and low-quality shelter: rental housing dynamics in Mathare Valley, Nairobi." *Environment and Urbanization* 32, no. 2 (2020): 503–520.
- Informal rental dynamics adjacent to formal student housing markets in Nairobi.
- [SAGE](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/0956247820942158)

**[Author].** "Issues and Challenges of Student Financing Systems in Africa." *International Journal of African Higher Education.* Boston College.
- Documents HESLB and equivalent failure modes — disbursement delays, weak enforcement, employer non-compliance.
- [Boston College Library](https://ejournals.bc.edu/index.php/ijahe/article/download/10250/8935/18216)

## What's missing in the literature

- **No peer-reviewed East African student-hostel-owner P&L study.** The economics of running a hostel in UG/KE/TZ/RW is undocumented in academic literature.
- **No published landlord-experience surveys** at scale in East Africa (the Chinhoyi study is the closest analogue).
- **No empirical evaluation of Makerere's 2023 inspection programme** or any equivalent — its effectiveness is unmeasured.
- **No academic study of HELB/HESLB delay impact on landlords** specifically (only on students).
- **No East African PBSA-financing feasibility study** equivalent to IFC's South Africa work.
- **No academic study of women hostel operators** as a distinct cohort.

These gaps are publication opportunities for any researcher in education economics, real estate, or development finance.
