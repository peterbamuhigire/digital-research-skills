# Owner-side: country-by-country breakdown

## Uganda

**Major universities driving demand:** Makerere, MUBS, Kyambogo, KIU, Mbarara University.

**Top owner pain points:**
1. **Government allowance delays** — 30+ MUBS evictions; allowances 446k–768k UGX/semester, often paid late or partially.
2. **Tax stack ≈35–40%** — KCCA Property Rates (6%), Local Hotel Tax, URA Rental Income Tax, VAT (18% above 150m), Corporate Income Tax (30%), staff NSSF/NHIF.
3. **Landlord & Tenant Act 2022 compliance** — written agreements above UGX 500k, 14-day documentation rule, ID verification, 90-day notice for rent increases, no self-help eviction (3 months' rent + damages penalty).
4. **Makerere 2023 inspection regime** — A/B/C grading, voluntary now but trending toward MOU-style mandatory enrollment.
5. **Aging stock + sanitation collapse** — Pearl View as cautionary tale; deferred maintenance increasingly visible.
6. **Bed-space informal competition** — Kampala "bed-space economy" undercuts formal hostels.

**Distinctive feature:** Uganda has the **most articulated regulatory framework** (Landlord & Tenant Act 2022) but **weak enforcement**. Owners face costs of compliance without offsetting benefits of a level playing field.

## Kenya

**Major universities driving demand:** UoN, Kenyatta, Strathmore, JKUAT, Moi, KEMU.

**Top owner pain points:**
1. **HELB delays** — student loan disbursements late, students "on brink of eviction," landlords absorb arrears.
2. **PPP existential threat** — Kenyatta University 10,000-bed Africa Integras concession; UoN 4,000-bed Mamlaka project. Both feature **university-collected rent through tuition** — competitive moat private operators cannot match.
3. **Rent control via student strikes** — UoN cut hostel fees by 50% (Sh43k → 21.5k single, 15.5k double) after Sept 2024 strike. Resets the regional ceiling.
4. **Tax stack** — Rental Income Tax 7.5% (eff. 1 Jan 2024) + NSSF (6%, capped 72k) + SHIF (2.75%) + PAYE + Affordable Housing Levy. Non-compliance: KSh 2m fine / 3 years' jail.
5. **Landlord & Tenant Bill 2021** — proposes inflation-capped rent increases, 12-month frequency limit, 90-day notice.
6. **Strict eviction process** — court-ordered only, 30/60-day notices, criminal liability for forcible entry (Penal Code §194).
7. **Insecurity in off-campus areas** (Kahawa Sukari, Roysambu) — landlord liability exposure.

**Distinctive feature:** Kenya has the **most aggressive PPP pipeline** in EA. Within 5km of UoN or Kenyatta, the next decade's market dynamics are set.

## Tanzania

**Major universities driving demand:** UDSM, Mzumbe, KIUT, SUA, University of Dodoma.

**Top owner pain points:**
1. **HESLB delays** — "two months to get accounts right and weeks to receive money." No employer ever punished for non-compliance.
2. **Spiralling rents in Dar** — highest in EA, driven by general housing shortage.
3. **3m-unit national housing deficit, +200k/year** — supply expansion is structural opportunity but capital costs gate it.
4. **Land tenure complexity** — academic literature on Tanzania highlights "weak tenure security" and "high costs of urban land."
5. **Limited public coverage of hostel-owner pain** — significant research gap (likely underreporting).

**Distinctive feature:** Tanzania has the **largest underlying housing deficit** but the **weakest published owner-side data**. Both an opportunity and a research gap.

## Rwanda

**Major university driving demand:** University of Rwanda (Huye, Kigali, Busogo, Nyagatare campuses).

**Top owner pain points:**
1. **2025 demand spike** — Huye 6,000 newcomers vs 3,000 beds; "23% undergrad coverage." Vice-Chancellor: "simply doesn't have enough rooms."
2. **Government mobilising Private Sector Federation** to encourage construction — opportunity if structured well, threat if government later subsidises supply.
3. **Inflated rents, few vacancies** — short-term landlord upside, but reputation damage if quality drops.
4. **Female-safety expectations** — student & parent demand premiumising secure properties; landlords investing in CCTV, fencing.
5. **Limited industry-owner forums** — under-organised relative to UG/KE.

**Distinctive feature:** Rwanda's market is **earliest-stage and most policy-coordinated**. A well-structured PBSA finance vehicle launched here could capture share before the market matures.

## Burundi & South Sudan

**Coverage:** Almost none in the source set.

**Inferred state:**
- Smaller HE sectors → smaller hostel market
- Likely informal-dominant
- Direct-research opportunity for any operator considering regional expansion

## Cross-country pattern

Every country surveyed has the same five owner pain dynamics:
1. Government-payment delays (allowance / loan structure varies)
2. Capital cost ≥ 18% locally
3. Tax stack growing
4. Reputational viral risk
5. No industry trade body with bargaining power

The countries diverge on:
- **Regulatory maturity:** UG (most articulated) > KE (PPP-driven) > TZ ≈ RW > BI / SS
- **PPP threat:** KE > UG ≈ RW > TZ > BI / SS
- **Research data availability:** UG > KE > TZ > RW > BI / SS

Any cross-border owner platform should start with **Uganda + Kenya**, since they have both the deepest pain documentation and the most regulatory hooks to anchor a value proposition.
