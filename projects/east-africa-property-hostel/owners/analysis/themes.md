# Owner-side theme taxonomy

Two top-level categories: **operational** (running the building day-to-day) and **business** (running it as a business). Each sub-theme tagged with prevalence and severity.

## A. Operational pain

| # | Sub-theme | Prevalence | Severity | Key evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A1 | Government allowance / loan delays (HELB, HESLB, UG allowances) | Universal where govt-sponsored students exist | Critical — direct cashflow hit | 30+ MUBS evictions; "2 months to get accounts right" (HESLB) |
| A2 | Private student rent default | Universal | High | Mid-semester dropouts; documented arrears |
| A3 | Property damage by tenants | Universal | Medium | Bedbugs, broken fixtures, theft |
| A4 | Drug / alcohol / party violations | High | High — legal liability | 6-country study: 10.5% illicit drug use |
| A5 | Violent incidents on premises | Documented | Critical — legal/PR catastrophic | Makerere murder; KIU stabbing |
| A6 | Illegal sub-letting / multiple occupancy | High in informal sector | Medium | Bed-space economy in Kampala/Nairobi/Dar |
| A7 | Vacancy & seasonality (4-month cycles) | Universal | High — 2–4 months no income | 80–100% annual turnover global benchmark |
| A8 | Utility cost volatility (power, water) | Universal | High | UGX 756/unit; Dar/Nairobi rationing |
| A9 | Maintenance burden (plumbing, sewerage) | Universal | High | Pearl View collapse; Kyambogo facility gaps |
| A10 | Staff turnover, theft, abuse | High | Medium | Caretaker harassment cases; hotel-staff theft |
| A11 | Compliance burden for staff (NSSF, SHIF, PAYE) | Universal in formal sector | High | KSh 2m fines / 3 yrs jail for non-compliance |
| A12 | Security incidents & liability | Universal | Critical | Kenya 2024 dorm fire (21 dead) |
| A13 | Insurance gaps | Widespread | Critical | "No guaranteed minimum" (CrossBoundary) |
| A14 | Reputational risk via TikTok / Twitter | Universal | Critical | "Golden 15 Minutes" of PR |

## Cross-cutting risk multipliers

- **Small operators (<50 beds)** carry disproportionate weight on A11, B1, B2, B5, B6 — they cannot afford compliance professionals, lose access to formal credit, struggle with tax stack.
- **Operators with >70% government-sponsored student mix** carry extra weight on A1, A7 — they are policy-vulnerable.
- **Operators within 5km of a PPP-target campus** carry extra weight on B8.
- **Operators in Kampala / Nairobi / Dar** face more A11, B5, B6 enforcement than rural counterparts.
- **Older buildings (10+ years)** carry extra weight on A9 (maintenance) and A13 (insurance) — most stock surveyed by Makerere 2023 was already aging.

## Severity-weighted top 5 (for product strategy)

1. **A1 — Loan delays** (universal, critical, recurring)
2. **B5/B6 — Tax & compliance burden** (universal, critical, growing)
3. **B1 — Capital cost** (universal, critical, structural)
4. **A14 — Reputational viral risk** (universal, critical, instantaneous)
5. **B8 — PPP competition** (geographic, critical, expanding)

## What's not yet mapped

- Rural / smaller-town hostel operator pain (most coverage is urban)
- Women-led hostel operations as a distinct segment
- Faith-based / mission-affiliated hostels (likely different cost structure)
- Multi-property operators vs single-property (likely very different pain profiles)
- Successor / inheritance dynamics — many EA hostels are family assets transitioning generations
