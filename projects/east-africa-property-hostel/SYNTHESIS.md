# Cross-cohort synthesis — east-africa-property-hostel

Compiled 2026-04-25 by the orchestrator (not delegated). Reads across all four cohort `analysis/themes.md` files plus their `research/pain-points-report.md` files to surface shared, cascade, symmetric, and mutually-reinforcing pains.

> **Provenance reminder:** Every claim in this synthesis traces back to a specific cohort file. The `evidence-discipline` skill applies — synthesis is allowed; fabrication is not. Any claim here that doesn't have an underlying claim in a cohort file is a bug.

## 1. Shared pains — same problem, multiple cohorts

The strongest signal in the corpus. When the same problem hits two or more cohorts, the underlying broken system is the leverage point.

| Pain | Cohorts hit | Mechanism | Strongest evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Deposit theft** | tenants + students | Landlord keeps deposit citing vague "damages"; 30-day refund mandate ignored | Daily Monitor Aug 2023 — 80% UG non-refund |
| **Broker / fake-listing scams** | tenants + students | Brokers extract viewing fees / multi-deposits on units they don't control | Spectrum Real Estate Solutions UG — 30% encounter fake listings |
| **Fragmented online platforms** | tenants + landlords | No platform has dominant share; stale listings; verification absent | Property24, BuyRent, Real Muloodi all <10k listings each |
| **Discrimination at letting** | tenants + landlords (defending) | Single women, refugees, PWDs face blanket exclusion | Medium / Daily Nation — 90% single-women rejection (single source — flagged) |
| **Eviction-without-due-process** | tenants + students | Self-help eviction (lockouts, furniture removal, power-cut) despite UG 2022 ban | UG L&T Act 2022 + tenants/research/pain-points-report.md C4 |
| **Caretaker bribery / harassment** | tenants + students | Caretaker extracts side payments; espionage to landlord / hostel-owner; documented sexual-favour requests | Olympia Hostel 2019; Daily Nation tenant reporting |
| **Statute exists, enforcement absent** | tenants + landlords + students + owners | Legal framework on paper, <30% awareness among tenants, <10% enforcement reach | UG L&T Act 2022, KE Bill 2021, Makerere 2023 inspection — all four cohorts experience this |

## 2. Cascade pains — A's failure causes B's pain

These are directional. Treating only one end is half a fix.

| Source pain | Source cohort | Target pain | Target cohort | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| HELB / HESLB / UG allowance delay | (gov) | Student can't pay rent | students | Loan disbursement late |
| Student can't pay rent | students | Landlord cashflow crisis → tenant eviction | owners → students | 30+ MUBS evictions case |
| Tenant default | tenants | Landlord turn to broker WhatsApp blacklists | landlords → tenants | Discrimination amplifies (single mothers blacklisted by landlord network) |
| Tenant strike → fee cut (UoN 2024) | students | Private hostel margins compressed | owners | Halved fees reset regional ceiling |
| KCCA / Nairobi water failure | (utility) | Tenant pays full rent for half-service | tenants | NCWSC 250M-litre/day deficit |
| Landlord defers maintenance | landlords | Tenant lives with sewage / pest / leaks | tenants | Pearl View Hostel; muzigos generally |
| State-led demolitions | (state) | Mass eviction with negligible compensation | tenants | Nairobi 2024 riparian — 20,000 displaced, KSh 10,000 each |
| Currency depreciation | (macro) | Imported building material +30-50% | landlords + owners | KSh -26.8%; passed partially to tenant rent |
| Tribunal backlog | (state) | Both sides resort to informal "remedies" | landlords + tenants | Self-help eviction, deposit theft become rational |

## 3. Symmetric pains — same feature, opposite valence

Two-sided products must navigate, not just solve.

| Feature | Good for | Bad for |
|---|---|---|
| Eviction speed (RW 3–5m, KE 7–11m) | Landlord recovery in fast jurisdictions | Tenant security in fast jurisdictions |
| Distress-for-rent (KE still operative) | Landlord rent recovery | Tenant property security |
| Inflation-cap rent control (KE Bill 2021) | Tenant affordability | Landlord margin in high-inflation periods |
| Mandatory written agreements (UG L&T Act >UGX 500k) | Tenant statutory protection | Small-landlord compliance burden |
| Verified-listings marketplace (hypothetical) | Tenant scam protection | Bad-actor landlord exclusion (if poor screening) |
| Tenant credit history (hypothetical) | Tenant counter-signal vs discrimination | Privacy exposure for tenant; DPA 2019 risk |
| 80% deposit non-refund | Landlord cashflow | Tenant accumulated capital loss |

## 4. Mutually reinforcing pains — A's coping behaviour worsens B's pain

These are the hardest to break out of. Coordination problems.

- **Tenant withholds last month's rent** ("eating the deposit") → **landlord stops refunding deposits** → next tenant withholds → cycle deepens
- **Landlord blacklists "problem" tenants on WhatsApp** → **single women / refugees / PWDs blacklisted by stereotype** → landlords double down on blacklists as the only screening tool → discrimination self-reinforces
- **Tenants underreport income to negotiate rent** → **landlords inflate quoted rent** → tenants underreport more → opacity escalates
- **Owners defer maintenance** to preserve margin against tax + currency pressure → **stock degrades** → tenant willingness-to-pay drops → margin pressure increases → maintenance deferred further (Pearl View, Akamwesi)
- **HESLB collects only 62% of matured loans** → government tightens new disbursements → students face delayed allowances → hostel owners absorb the gap → MUBS-style mass evictions

## 6. Highest-leverage interventions, ranked

Combining cohort-coverage + severity:

1. **Deposit escrow** — sharpest single pain (80% UG non-refund), widest cohort hit (tenants + students), cleanest legal hook (UG L&T Act 2022; KE Tribunal Case E007/2024)
2. **Verified-listings marketplace** — largest fraud volume (30% UG fake-listing rate), three-cohort coverage
3. **Allowance-bridge financing** — most acute cashflow crisis (3-month MUBS allowance gap), institutional buy-in plausible
4. **Tenant credit + landlord screening twin** — highest leverage on the discrimination loop, hardest to break
5. **Compliance-as-a-service for small landlords** — rising regulatory burden, low willingness-to-pay but compounding penalty risk

## 7. Contradictions / unresolved across cohorts

These are findings the report should expose, not paper over.

- **CRB adoption among landlords:** "<5%" (landlords/research) vs. "informal references preferred" — both are technically compatible, but the CRB number is anecdotal industry estimate, not measured. **Mark "(estimated)"** before final.
- **90% single-women rejection rate** — single source (Medium synthesis citing Daily Nation). Flag as **needing independent confirmation** before publication.
- **Eviction timelines** — composed from journalism, not court-statistics datasets. Mark **"(synthesised — no single authoritative dataset)"**.
- **Acorn Holdings FY'25 financials** — rich but not yet verified against NSE filings or annual report. **Verify before final**.
- **"Kenyatta U Africa Integras PPP stalled"** — agent flagged "no completion updates found" but this is absence of evidence, not evidence of stall. **Re-search before stating definitively**.

## 8. Structural gaps surfaced by cross-cohort comparison

These gaps appear in multiple cohort files — they are real research-design problems, not search-effort problems.

- **Insurance loss ratios for residential / hostel cohort** — IRA KE / IRA UG don't disaggregate
- **Court-eviction enforcement statistics** — judicial branches don't aggregate
- **Women-led landlords / hostel operators** — no cohort-level data anywhere
- **Reddit / Facebook closed-group verbatim quotes** — ToS / API constraints
- **Refugee-tenant household-level survey** — UNHCR / UN-Habitat have framework, not numbers
- **Disability-housing accessibility hard data** — single-source 95% estimate
- **Informal-operator P&L** — off-record by definition

These are **engine-wide gaps**, not project-specific. Future projects should expect to encounter the same structural absences in adjacent domains.
