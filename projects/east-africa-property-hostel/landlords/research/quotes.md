# Landlord-side & stakeholder quotes

Verbatim quotes from public sources. The landlord voice is **quieter** than the tenant voice in EA media — most evidence comes from industry analysts, regulators, and a handful of named operators. The Reddit sub-agent flagged that real-time tenant/landlord quote scraping was constrained by Reddit TOS / API rate limits; future passes with PRAW + auth would yield richer raw quotes.

## Industry analysts

> Kenyan developers face "commercial financing rates of 18–22%, while foreign developers can access capital at 4–8%."
— IFC / World Bank Africa Housing research

> Sub-Saharan Africa mortgage rates "reaching upwards from 25 percent in some countries."
— World Bank, *Stocktaking of the Housing Sector*

> "The rental market is ripe for landlord-centred digital revolution."
— Business Daily Africa, 2024 commentary on Kenya's PropTech gap

> "Most lands near these universities are already occupied; thus, developers must look elsewhere, further away from the campuses."
— CrossBoundary Advisory

## Regulatory / institutional

> Uganda's Landlord & Tenant Act 2022 requires written agreements for tenancies above UGX 500,000; allows tenant compensation of three months' rent + damages for unlawful self-help eviction.
— ULII / CEPIL Uganda summary

> Kenya's Tribunal Case E007 of 2024 established that Rent Tribunals hear security-deposit refund claims within 60 days under CAP. 301.
— Kenyalaw.org

> *Komakech & 7 Ors v Ayaa & Anor (2018)* — eviction requires court order; forcible removal is unlawful even after lease expiry; statutory tenancy protects holdover tenants.
— ULII Uganda

## Operators

> "People are the best opportunity for business, and the biggest challenge."
— Edward Kirathe, CEO Acorn Holdings

## Press / journalism

> "80 percent of tenants have never gotten a refund of their security deposit from their previous landlords even when the house has been left in good condition."
— Daily Monitor Uganda investigation

> Landlords in Kampala and Nairobi report eviction processes typically running **6–11 months** in magistrate court.
— synthesised from multiple property-section pieces (Daily Monitor, Business Daily, The Standard)

## Pattern from informal landlord WhatsApp groups (paraphrased)

The sub-agent did not extract verbatim quotes from landlord WhatsApp / Telegram groups (closed, encrypted, ToS-protected). Recurring themes reported by participants in published interviews:

- "Tenants always have an excuse the week before rent."
- "Once they hold over past notice, you've lost a year."
- "Caretakers are 80% of the problem — staff theft is worse than tenant theft."
- "If you take them to court, you'll spend more on the lawyer than the rent."

These are paraphrased from second-hand journalism and should be replaced with verbatim primary-source quotes in a future research pass.

## Synthesis

The asymmetry is striking: there is no equivalent of *Campus Bee* for landlords; no Reddit forum where landlords aggregate complaints; no AREA-Uganda-equivalent in Kenya for the operator-of-existing-stock segment. **The landlord voice is structurally under-represented in EA public discourse** — which itself is a finding, and a content / community opportunity (see `opportunities/product-ideas.md` #8).
