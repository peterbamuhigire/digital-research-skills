# Landlord-side theme taxonomy — residential rentals

Two top-level categories: **demand-side** (finding & screening tenants, collecting rent) and **supply-side** (running the building, regulation, finance). Each tagged by prevalence and severity.

## A. Demand-side pain (tenants in / paying / out)

| # | Sub-theme | Prevalence | Severity | Key evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A1 | Long time-on-market for vacancies | Universal | High | 8–14 weeks mid-Nairobi; 10–20 weeks Kampala secondary |
| A2 | Broker commission cost / dependence | Universal | Medium | 1 month rent in KE; UG zero-commission emerging |
| A3 | Fragmented online platforms | Universal | Medium | No platform with dominant share |
| A4 | Stale listings / bait-and-switch | High | Medium | Property24 KE doesn't auto-remove |
| A5 | Tenant-ID verification weakness | Universal | High | Counterfeit IDs persist; no centralised real-time validator |
| A6 | CRB adoption gap (<5%) | Kenya specifically | High | Cost prohibitive; informal references preferred |
| A7 | Bad-tenant blacklist informality | High (KE, UG) | Medium — legal exposure | Landlord WhatsApp groups; DPA 2019 risk |
| A8 | Discrimination by landlord (tribe, nationality, religion, occupation, marital status) | Universal | High — legal & reputational | UTAK reports; Constitutional articles unenforced |
| A9 | Cash-dominant collection (25–30% KE informal) | High | High — fraud / theft risk | M-Pesa 60–70% urban only |
| A10 | Default rates 10–25% informal segments | High | Critical | Rising eviction filings 2024–25 |
| A11 | Eviction delays | Universal | Critical | KE 7–11 months; UG 4–18; TZ 8–12; RW 3–5 |
| A12 | Rent-escalation disputes | Universal | High | 90-day notice rules ignored; tribunal backlog |
| A13 | Holdover tenants | High | High | No self-help permitted; 6+ months process |
| A14 | Pastor / bar / NGO conversion of flats | High in KE / UG | High — code violations + slow eviction | Religious-freedom defence stalls cases |

## B. Supply-side pain (running the building / business)

| # | Sub-theme | Prevalence | Severity | Key evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| B1 | Pest infestation (bedbugs, cockroaches, termites) | Universal | High | 2–5% of rental income on pest control |
| B2 | Utility outages (KPLC / UMEME / TANESCO) | Universal | High | 2–4 outages/month; 5–7 day water cuts |
| B3 | Plumbing / electrical / roofing maintenance | Universal | High | KSh hundreds of thousands per cycle |
| B4 | Caretaker / askari turnover, theft, abuse | High | High | 6–12 month avg tenure; 15–25% blocks affected by theft |
| B5 | Insurance penetration <1% | Universal | Critical when triggered | 5–10% uninsured loss / year |
| B6 | Mortgage penetration only 3.6% (KE) | Universal | High | 12–22% commercial rates |
| B7 | Currency depreciation → material costs +30–50% | Universal | High | KSh -26.8% in 2022–23 |
| B8 | Vacancy + turnover cost | Universal | High | KSh 30–80k turnover per unit; 3–5% return erosion |
| B9 | Regulatory uncertainty (UG L&T Act 2022, KE Bill 2021 pending) | Universal | High | Compliance retrofitting expensive |
| B10 | Tax stack (KE 7.5% RIT, UG 6% rates + RIT + VAT, payroll) | Universal | High | Effective burden 25–40% revenue |
| B11 | Building permits / change-of-use / NEMA | Universal | Medium-High | KSh 50K–2m, 4–16 weeks |
| B12 | Distress for Rent — banned UG, still allowed KE (auctioneer 20-30% fee) | KE / UG specific | Medium — rarely used | Auctioneer cost > recovery |
| B13 | Court backlog & legal cost | Universal | High | Tribunal 4–6m; courts 6–18m |
| B14 | Land fraud / "selling air" / multiple titles | Universal | Critical | KE titling reform incomplete |
| B15 | Election-cycle politicised mass evictions / squats | UG, KE | Critical episodically | Nairobi informal settlements |
| B16 | Death of tenant on premises | Rare but unbounded | Critical when triggered | No clear legal framework |

## Cross-cutting risk multipliers

- **Small landlords (<10 units)** — disproportionately suffer A6, B5, B6, B10. Cannot afford CRB checks, insurance, professional management, full tax compliance.
- **Landlords in informal segments** — disproportionately suffer A9, A10, A11, B4, B15. Cash dominant; eviction harder; political risk live.
- **Landlords with imported-material stock (newer builds)** — disproportionately suffer B7. Older stock is FX-insulated.
- **Landlords without written agreements (UG below UGX 500k threshold)** — disproportionately suffer A11–A13. Statutory tenancy protections work against them.
- **Landlords running residential-commercial mixes** — disproportionately suffer A14 (church / bar conversions).

## Severity-weighted top 5 for product strategy

1. **A11 — Eviction delays** (universal, critical, structural)
2. **A6 + A5 — Tenant screening blindness** (universal, high, addressable)
3. **A10 — Default rates** (high, critical, recurring)
4. **B5 — Insurance gap** (universal, critical when triggered)
5. **A11 + A2 — Vacancy & broker cost** (universal, high, ongoing)

These five define where any landlord-facing product must focus to earn willingness-to-pay.

## What's not yet mapped

- **Female landlords** as a distinct cohort (same gap as in `owners/` research)
- **Landlords in informal settlements (Mathare, Kibera, Kawangware, Bwaise)** — different operating logic, almost no published data
- **Multi-property family-owned portfolios** — succession & inheritance dynamics
- **Diaspora-owned units** (remittance-funded) — managed remotely, distinct pain
- **Co-living / serviced-apartment operators** as a distinct segment
- **NGO / mission-affiliated landlord** — distinct cost & rent structures
