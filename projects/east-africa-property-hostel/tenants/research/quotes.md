# Tenant-side & stakeholder quotes

Voices from tenants, journalists, regulators, NGOs, and academics. Use for empathy maps, persona work, and pitch decks.

## The headline statistic

> "80% of tenants have never gotten a refund of their security deposit from their previous landlords even when the house has been left in good condition. They either get apologies or half of it."
— Daily Monitor Uganda, August 2023 investigation

## Tenant voice — direct & paraphrased

> "I have viewed nine houses this month. Every broker collected viewing fee. Two of the listings were fake — the photos were stolen from a real listing in another estate."
— Kampala renter, Spectrum Real Estate Solutions Uganda survey (paraphrased)

> "[Landlord] entered the unit without notice and asked for a sexual favour."
— Daily Nation reporting on caretaker harassment in Nairobi

> "We pay rent for water that doesn't come. The landlord shrugs."
— Mathare resident, paraphrased from Mathare Resident Association advocacy

## Vulnerable groups

> Single women are rejected by an estimated 90% of landlords compared with single men of similar income — landlords cite assumptions about morality, sex work, or "noise from visitors."
— Medium / Daily Nation research synthesis

> Refugees in Nairobi (Somali, Congolese, S. Sudanese) face systematic landlord exclusion citing "security risk" — pushing an estimated 50,000–100,000 informal Somali tenants into Eastleigh and overcrowded informal settlements.
— Synthesised from UNHCR Kenya & UN-Habitat

> ~95% of Kenyan rental stock is non-accessible to persons with disabilities. Housing eats 50–80% of disposable income for tenants with disabilities.
— Synthesis from disability-housing advocacy reporting

## Mathare Valley — the academic anchor

> 87% of Mathare residents earn below the Kenyan minimum wage. **80.9% of tenants are in rent arrears** at any given moment.
— Huchzermeyer, Bocquier, Beguy, *Environment and Urbanization* 32(2), 2020

## Kigali

> 79% of Kigali residents live in informal settlements. 54% earn between $38 and $225 per month — the population for whom market-rate housing is structurally unaffordable.
— International Growth Centre (IGC) / Bloomberg 2024

## Stakeholder / political

> "Skyrocketing hostel fees forcing students into slums."
— Senator Edwin Sifuna, Kenya (also relevant to general tenants in adjacent informal settlements)

> The 2024 Nairobi riparian demolitions displaced 20,000+ residents from Kibera, Mathare, and Mukuru. Compensation: KSh 10,000 each. Pretext: flood mitigation.
— Synthesis from Daily Nation / Standard / Hakijamii reporting

## Regulatory framing

> Uganda's Landlord & Tenant Act 2022 caps deposits at one month's rent and mandates 30-day refund with itemised deductions. Distress for rent is **abolished**. Unlawful self-help eviction entitles the tenant to 3 months' rent compensation + damages.
— ULII / CEPIL Uganda summary

> Kenya's Tribunal Case E007 of 2024 mandates 60-day deposit refunds under CAP 301. Muhanda v LP Holdings (2025) gave Small Claims Court jurisdiction.
— Kenyalaw.org

## Awareness gap

> Fewer than 30% of EA tenants know their rights under the relevant statute. Most landlord behaviour goes unchallenged because the tenant doesn't know it's unlawful.
— Synthesised across Hakijamii, CEPIL, Justice Centres Uganda outreach data

## Synthesis

The tenant voice is **loud in journalism but quiet in policy**. There is no formal tenant union in any EA country — only advocacy NGOs, legal-aid centres, and slum-federation savings groups. This asymmetry against organised landlord WhatsApp networks is a structural cause of the enforcement gap.
